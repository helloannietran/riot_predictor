<p><b>Ōdate</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">大館市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ōdate-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> in <a href="/wiki/Akita_Prefecture" title="Akita Prefecture">Akita Prefecture</a>, Japan. As of February 2015, the city has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 74,862 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 82 persons per km². The total area is 913.22 square kilometres (352.60&#160;sq&#160;mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%8Cdate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ōdate is located in the mountains of northern Akita Prefecture, with the <a href="/wiki/Ou_Mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Ou Mountains">Ou Mountains</a> on the east and <a href="/wiki/Aomori_Prefecture" title="Aomori Prefecture">Aomori Prefecture</a> to the north. The city is a basin surrounded by mountains on all sides. Part of the city is within the borders of the <a href="/wiki/Towada-Hachimantai_National_Park" title="Towada-Hachimantai National Park">Towada-Hachimantai National Park</a>. Much of the city area is covered in forest. Due to its inland location, the city is noted for its <a href="/wiki/Snow_country_(Japan)" title="Snow country (Japan)">heavy snowfall in winter</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%8Cdate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ōdate has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">Humid continental climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Dfa</i>) with large seasonal temperature differences, with warm to hot (and often humid) summers and cold (sometimes severely cold) winters. Precipitation is significant throughout the year, but is heaviest from August to October.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%8Cdate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The area of present-day Ōdate was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Dewa_Province" title="Dewa Province">Dewa Province</a>, although it remained outside of the control of the <a href="/wiki/Yamato_dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Yamato dynasty">Yamato court</a> until well into the <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, the area came under the control of the <a href="/wiki/Satake_clan" title="Satake clan">Satake clan</a>, who ruled the northern third of the province from <a href="/wiki/Kubota_Domain" title="Kubota Domain">Kubota Domain</a>, and who maintained a secondary fortification at <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%8Cdate_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ōdate Castle (page does not exist)">Ōdate Castle</a>. The castle was destroyed during the <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a>. After the start of the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>, the area became part of <a href="/wiki/Kitaakita_District,_Akita" title="Kitaakita District, Akita">Kitaakita District</a>, Akita Prefecture in 1878. During the Meiji period, the discovery of "black ore" (<a href="/wiki/Sphalerite" title="Sphalerite">sphalerite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Galena" title="Galena">galena</a> - a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Zinc" title="Zinc">zinc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lead" title="Lead">lead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silver</a>, and other precious metals), led to the development of numerous mines in the area, including the <a href="/wiki/Hanaoka_mine" title="Hanaoka mine">Hanaoka mine</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-Kiruna_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kiruna-2">[2]</a></sup> however, the deposits were depleted by the mid-<a href="/wiki/Showa_period" class="mw-redirect" title="Showa period">Showa period</a>.</p>
<p>On June 20, 2005, the towns of <a href="/wiki/Hinai,_Akita" title="Hinai, Akita">Hinai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tashiro,_Akita" title="Tashiro, Akita">Tashiro</a> (both from <a href="/wiki/Kitaakita_District,_Akita" title="Kitaakita District, Akita">Kitaakita District</a>) were merged into Ōdate, increasing its population (as of 2011) to 77,703 and the area of the city to 913.70&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (352.78&#160;sq&#160;mi).</p>
