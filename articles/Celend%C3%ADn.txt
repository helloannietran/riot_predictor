<p><b>Celendín</b> is a town in northern <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, capital of the province <a href="/wiki/Celend%C3%ADn_Province" title="Celendín Province">Celendín</a> in the region <a href="/wiki/Cajamarca_Region" title="Cajamarca Region">Cajamarca</a>.</p>
<p>During the first week of July 2012, four citizens protesting a mining project were killed by police forces under orders of the government led by Ollanta Humala.<sup id="cite_ref-lt_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lt-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celend%C3%ADn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The history of the City of Celendín begins in the colonial era, when it was founded and inhabited by some Spanish families. Then in the eighteenth century, Portuguese and Jewish settlers who first arrived in northern Brazil settled in the small region and then for mysterious reasons to Zelendin estate as tenants. With effort these first inhabitants managed to gather about 14010 <a href="/wiki/Pesos" class="mw-redirect" title="Pesos">pesos</a>, and then buy the property 'La Pura y Limpia Concepcion de Zelendin'. This event made people chose their first authorities, by vote, Lieutenant Colonel of the Portuguese Dragon Army Don <a href="/w/index.php?title=Raymundo_da_Pereyra&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Raymundo da Pereyra (page does not exist)">Raymundo da Pereyra</a> was elected on first vote, and Captain <a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_de_Burga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Juan de Burga (page does not exist)">Juan de Burga</a> was elected second vote; aldermen were elected lieutenants of surnames Pereyra, Segarra, Texada, Fronton, Dias y Araujo. As time went on few Chinese families migrated to Celendín and then people from different places.</p>
