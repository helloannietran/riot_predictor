<p><b>The NK Široki Brijeg- FK Sarajevo football riot</b> refers to the riot involving the football clubs <a href="/wiki/NK_%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg" title="NK Široki Brijeg">NK Široki Brijeg</a> (supported mostly by <a href="/wiki/Croats_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Croats of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Herzegovian Croat</a> fans<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) and <a href="/wiki/FK_Sarajevo" title="FK Sarajevo">FK Sarajevo</a> (supported mostly by <a href="/wiki/Bosniak" class="mw-redirect" title="Bosniak">Bosniak</a> fans) during a leg of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Premier_League_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="2009–10 Premier League of Bosnia and Herzegovina">2009</a> <a href="/wiki/Premier_League_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Premier League of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnian-Herzegovian Premier League</a> in the Herzegovian town of Široki Brijeg. During the riot members of <a href="/wiki/Horde_Zla" title="Horde Zla">Horde Zla</a> ("Legions of Evil", a FK Sarajevo supporters group) and <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0kripari" title="Škripari">Škripari</a> (a NK Široki Brijeg supporters group) confronted each other in alterations which subsequently spilled out to the majority of the town. Horde Zla burned cars and demolished shops while Škripari and residents of Široki Brijeg stoned Horde Zla buses and shot at supporters. Horde Zla also claim multiple counts of severe police brutality which led to the death of Horde Zla member <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vedran_Pulji%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vedran Puljić (page does not exist)">Vedran Puljić</a> from gunshot wounds.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-SD-nogomet_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SD-nogomet-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_riots">The riots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_NK_%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg%E2%80%93FK_Sarajevo_football_riots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The riots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are conflicting reports about who started the violence. Horde Zla accused local residents and police for their mistreatment on their away to the match, saying that the incident was planned by local politicians and that their buses were separated and parked too far from the stadium which left them open to stoning and attacks. They also claim that initially there were only 30 local policemen present, and they did nothing to prevent the violence.<sup id="cite_ref-isn.ethz.ch_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isn.ethz.ch-5">[5]</a></sup> Local police and residents said Horde Zla fans were the first to attack.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> According to local police officials, most of the regional police had earlier been sent to the nearby city of Mostar to prevent possible violence during the match between the rival <a href="/wiki/H%C5%A0K_Zrinjski_Mostar" title="HŠK Zrinjski Mostar">Zrinjski</a> and <a href="/wiki/FK_Vele%C5%BE" class="mw-redirect" title="FK Velež">Velež</a> clubs scheduled for the same day thus leaving the match in Široki Brijeg relatively ill secured.<sup id="cite_ref-isn.ethz.ch_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isn.ethz.ch-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some 500 fans of the Sarajevo football club were present during the match in <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg" title="Široki Brijeg">Široki Brijeg</a><sup id="cite_ref-isn.ethz.ch_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isn.ethz.ch-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> as well as between 150 and 200 members of the Red Army (supporters of FK Velež closely aligned with Horde Zla).<sup id="cite_ref-sarajevo-x.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sarajevo-x.com-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sarajevo-x.com_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sarajevo-x.com-8">[8]</a></sup> According to Croatian media, members of Horde Zla have a history of starting riots in nearby <a href="/wiki/Posu%C5%A1je" title="Posušje">Posušje</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-poskok.info_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poskok.info-10">[10]</a></sup> and that Horde Zla were involved in similar riots before. In September 1990 they had a conflict in Belgrade and they stabbed 2 members of <a href="/wiki/Grobari" title="Grobari">Grobari</a> (supporters of <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizan" title="FK Partizan">FK Partizan</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> While Škripari have been accused by Bosnian media of harboring far-right Ustaše sympathies including displaying the Nazi swastika in their stands on multiple occasions.</p>
<p>31 people were injured in the riots, 6 of them heavily.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> One member of Horde, named Vedran Puljić (a <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo_Canton" title="Sarajevo Canton">Sarajevo</a> Croat),<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> was shot and subsequently died.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> According to Zlatko Galić, the chief of <a href="/wiki/West_Herzegovina_Canton" title="West Herzegovina Canton">West Herzegovina Canton</a> police, Vedran Puljić had a record for multiple violations related to hooliganism.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Police arrested Oliver Knezović as a suspect of shooting Vedran Puljić. Knezović is believed to be a former member of Kažnjenička Bojna unit of <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Defence_Council" title="Croatian Defence Council">HVO</a>. He reportedly fired at the FK Sarajevo supporters using a Kalashnikov automatic rifle.<sup id="cite_ref-balkaninsight.com_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-balkaninsight.com-15">[15]</a></sup> Knezović escaped from prison only hours after he was apprehended.<sup id="cite_ref-balkaninsight.com_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-balkaninsight.com-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Eight Široki Brijeg policemen were detained for allowing the escape of Oliver Knezović.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Oliver Knezović fled to <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>, Croatia, where he would not be extradited to the authorities of <a href="/wiki/Bosnia" class="mw-redirect" title="Bosnia">Bosnia</a> and Herzegovina, as there is no agreement on this issue between the two countries. He gave an interview for a local TV network, asserting his innocence.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> After the interview Knezović surrendered to a Zagreb police station with his lawyer.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-19">[19]</a></sup> An autopsy revealed that Puljić was killed by a bullet from a police pistol.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Knezović reportedly had been shooting from an <a href="/wiki/AK_47" class="mw-redirect" title="AK 47">AK 47</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Citing unnamed police sources, some media reported that Knezović took the pistol from an officer before shooting Puljić.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-24">[24]</a></sup> Knezović said that he had taken the pistol but returned it to the policeman before the shooting. He later came to the police station, but escaped after claiming that the police were looking to arrest him for not only shooting at members of Horde Zla (which he does not dispute he did) but also for the murder of Vedran Puljić (which he claims was committed by someone else.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-19">[19]</a></sup> The policeman Dragan Vujović was also taken into custody in relation to the murder of Puljić. Citing unnamed police sources, some media reports claimed Vujović is being investigated as an accomplice to murder.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-24">[24]</a></sup> Other media reports said that Vujović's hands tested positive for gunpowder residue, which he purportedly claimed was due to his coming into contact with the large quantities of fireworks used that day. Some reports say that the policeman has failed a polygraph test in relation to the event.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some members of Horde zla were arrested and put in custody. But after Horde Zla spokespeople claimed they had reports that those members were being mistreated in prison the Federal Minister Of Justice Mirsad Kebo had them transferred to Sarajevo and released.<sup id="cite_ref-poskok.info_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poskok.info-10">[10]</a></sup> This move was met with disapproval by citizens of Široki Brijeg.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_NK_%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg%E2%80%93FK_Sarajevo_football_riots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".C5.A0iroki_Brijeg">Široki Brijeg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_NK_%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg%E2%80%93FK_Sarajevo_football_riots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Široki Brijeg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the following days many citizens had gathered in front of the West Herzegovina county headquarters. They blamed the violence on Horde Zla. Miro Kraljević, the mayor, addressed the audience giving them his support. The official representative of Škripari read a list of requests to the authorities of West Herzegovina county.</p>
<p>The protester left after a speech by Stanko Škorba, the president of the Union of handicapped people from the Homeland war. Škorba told them that the ministry of internal affairs has accepted to look into their requests. Although, he said, that does not mean that the Ministry necessarily sees these complaints as valid.</p>
<p>The council of Široki Brijeg also held an extraordinary meeting. The counselors made a declaration denouncing the violence that occurred.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The protests were repeated the same week. There were between 13 and 15<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> thousand protesters. Besides citizens of Široki Brijeg the fans of Croat dominated clubs from Mostar, Tomislavgrad, Livno, Žepče, Kiseljak, Posavina and a number of people from Croatia attended. Representatives of Škripari demanded the resignation of Mirsad Kebo and Zvonimir Jukić.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_NK_%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg%E2%80%93FK_Sarajevo_football_riots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sarajevo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a>, members of Horde Zla also organized city wide demonstrations. They were joined by fans of Velež, Željezničar, Čelik, Rudar (Kakanj), Travnik, Sloboda and many other, mainly Bosniak, clubs as well as over 10 000 citizens.</p>
<p>They alleged that the causes for violence started even before they entered the town of Široki Brijeg in the form of provocations by local residents shouting ethnic slurs at them and gross negligence by the Široki Brijeg police which split them up into small unprotected groups and completely ignored any misconduct on the side of the Škripari up to and including doing nothing to stop the Škripari from hurling rocks at them. The supporters of many other Bosnian clubs present during the demonstrations have also reported that they were subject to similar ethnic provocations and willful police neglect when visiting Western Herzegovina.</p>
<p>They also expressed shock at the fact that as soon as physical confrontations broke out between them and the Škripari, the police started attacking members of Horde Zla in unison with Škripari and other local residents instead of doing their job of separating them. They demanded a thorough investigation into the misconduct of the Široki Brijeg police force which later confirmed that multiple policemen have been aiding and abetting assaults on members of Horde Zla including, in the case of policeman Dragan Vujović, being accomplices to murder. They also accused the police of ignoring local residents who had been shooting automatic weapons at the members of Horde Zla as in the case of Oliver Knezević who can be seen holding an AK-47 while being ignored by a nearby police officer on surveillance footage.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
