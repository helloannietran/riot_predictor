<p>The <b>1991 Soviet coup d'état attempt</b>, also known as the <b>August Putsch</b> or <b>August Coup</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Августовский путч</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Russian" title="Romanization of Russian">tr.</a></small> <i>Avgustovsky Putch</i>), was an attempt by members of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>'s government to take control of the country from Soviet president <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup</a> leaders were hard-line members of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Communist Party of the Soviet Union">Communist Party of the Soviet Union</a> (CPSU) who were opposed to Gorbachev's reform program and the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Sovereign_States" title="Union of Sovereign States">new union treaty</a> that he had negotiated which decentralised much of the central government's power to the republics. They were opposed, mainly in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, by a short but effective campaign of <a href="/wiki/Civil_resistance" title="Civil resistance">civil resistance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kramer_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kramer-6">[6]</a></sup> Although the coup collapsed in only two days and Gorbachev returned to government, the event destabilised the Soviet Union and is widely considered to have contributed to both the demise of the CPSU and the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">dissolution of the Soviet Union</a>.</p>
<p>After the capitulation of the <a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">State Committee on the State of Emergency</a> (GKChP), popularly referred to as the "Gang of Eight", both the Supreme Court of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic</a> (RSFSR) and the President of the USSR Mikhail Gorbachev described their actions as a coup attempt.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since assuming power in 1985, Gorbachev had embarked on an ambitious program of reform, embodied in the twin concepts of <i><a href="/wiki/Perestroika" title="Perestroika">perestroika</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Glasnost" title="Glasnost">glasnost</a></i>, meaning economic/political restructuring and openness, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> These moves prompted resistance and suspicion on the part of hardline members of the <i><a href="/wiki/Nomenklatura" title="Nomenklatura">nomenklatura</a></i>. The reforms also unleashed some forces and movements that Gorbachev did not expect.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Specifically, <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">nationalist</a> agitation on the part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>'s non-<a href="/wiki/Russians" title="Russians">Russian</a> minorities grew, and there were fears that some or all of the <a href="/wiki/Republics_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Republics of the Soviet Union">union republics</a> might secede. In 1991, the Soviet Union was in a severe economic and political crisis. There were shortages of almost all products, and people had to stand in long <a href="/wiki/Queue_area" title="Queue area">lines</a> to buy even essential goods.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Estonian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Estonian Soviet Socialist Republic">Estonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Latvian Soviet Socialist Republic">Latvia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Lithuanian Soviet Socialist Republic">Lithuania</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Georgian Soviet Socialist Republic">Georgia</a> had already declared their independence from the Soviet Union.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In January 1991, there was an <a href="/wiki/January_Events_(Lithuania)" title="January Events (Lithuania)">attempt to return Lithuania to the Soviet Union by force</a>. About a week later, there was <a href="/wiki/The_Barricades" title="The Barricades">a similar attempt</a> by local pro-Soviet forces to overthrow the Latvian authorities. There were continuing armed ethnic conflicts in <a href="/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_War" title="Nagorno-Karabakh War">Nagorno Karabakh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgian%E2%80%93Ossetian_conflict" title="Georgian–Ossetian conflict">South Ossetia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Russia</a> <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_State_Sovereignty_of_the_Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Declaration of State Sovereignty of the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">declared its sovereignty</a> on 12 June 1990 and thereafter limited the application of Soviet laws, in particular the laws concerning finance and the economy, on Russian territory. The <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_Russia" title="Supreme Soviet of Russia">Supreme Soviet of the Russian SFSR</a> adopted laws which contradicted Soviet laws (the so-called <a href="/wiki/War_of_Laws" title="War of Laws">War of Laws</a>).</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union_referendum,_1991" title="Soviet Union referendum, 1991">unionwide referendum on 17 March 1991</a>, boycotted by the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_states" title="Baltic states">Baltic states</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Georgian Soviet Socialist Republic">Georgia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Moldavian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic">Moldova</a>, the majority of the residents of the rest of the republics expressed the desire to retain the renewed Soviet Union. Following negotiations, eight of the nine republics (except <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic">Ukraine</a>) approved the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Sovereign_States" title="Union of Sovereign States">New Union Treaty</a> with some conditions. The treaty would make the Soviet Union a federation of independent republics with a common president, foreign policy, and military. <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakh_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic">Kazakhstan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uzbek_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Uzbek Soviet Socialist Republic">Uzbekistan</a> were to sign the Treaty in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> on 20 August 1991.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation">Preparation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 11 December 1990, <a href="/wiki/KGB" title="KGB">KGB</a> Chairman <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Kryuchkov" title="Vladimir Kryuchkov">Vladimir Kryuchkov</a>, made a "call for order" over Central television in Moscow.<sup id="cite_ref-State_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-State-8">[8]</a></sup> That day, he asked two KGB officers<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> to prepare a plan of measures that could be taken in case a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a> was declared in the USSR. Later, Kryuchkov brought Soviet Defense Minister <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Yazov" title="Dmitry Yazov">Dmitry Yazov</a>, Internal Affairs Minister <a href="/wiki/Boris_Pugo" title="Boris Pugo">Boris Pugo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Premier of the Soviet Union">Premier</a> <a href="/wiki/Valentin_Pavlov" title="Valentin Pavlov">Valentin Pavlov</a>, Vice-President <a href="/wiki/Gennady_Yanayev" title="Gennady Yanayev">Gennady Yanayev</a>, Soviet Defense Council deputy chief <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Baklanov" title="Oleg Baklanov">Oleg Baklanov</a>, Gorbachev secretariat head <a href="/w/index.php?title=Valeriy_Boldin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Valeriy Boldin (page does not exist)">Valeriy Boldin</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%91%D0%BE%D0%BB%D0%B4%D0%B8%D0%BD,_%D0%92%D0%B0%D0%BB%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B8%D0%B9_%D0%98%D0%B2%D0%B0%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%87" class="extiw" title="ru:Болдин, Валерий Иванович">ru</a>)</span>, and <a href="/wiki/Secretariat_of_the_Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Secretariat of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union">CPSU Central Committee Secretary</a> <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Shenin" title="Oleg Shenin">Oleg Shenin</a> into the conspiracy.<sup id="cite_ref-91internalkgbreport_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91internalkgbreport-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NovayaGazeta51_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovayaGazeta51-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The members of the <a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">GKChP</a> hoped that Gorbachev could be persuaded to declare the state of emergency and to "restore order".</p>
<p>On 23 July 1991, a number of party functionaries and literati published in the hardline newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Sovetskaya_Rossiya" title="Sovetskaya Rossiya">Sovetskaya Rossiya</a></i> as a prime factor to an anti-Perestroika manifesto entitled <a href="/wiki/A_Word_to_the_People" title="A Word to the People">A Word to the People</a>.</p>
<p>Six days later, Gorbachev, <a href="/wiki/President_of_Russia" title="President of Russia">Russian President</a> <a href="/wiki/Boris_Yeltsin" title="Boris Yeltsin">Boris Yeltsin</a> and <a href="/wiki/President_of_Kazakhstan" title="President of Kazakhstan">Kazakh President</a> <a href="/wiki/Nursultan_Nazarbayev" title="Nursultan Nazarbayev">Nursultan Nazarbayev</a> discussed the possibility of replacing such hardliners as Pavlov, Yazov, Kryuchkov and Pugo with more liberal figures. Kryuchkov, who had placed Gorbachev under close surveillance as Subject 110 several months earlier, eventually got wind of the conversation.<sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Mitrokhin_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mitrokhin-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 4 August, Gorbachev went on holiday to his <a href="/wiki/Dacha" title="Dacha">dacha</a> in <a href="/wiki/Foros,_Crimea" title="Foros, Crimea">Foros, Crimea</a>. He planned to return to Moscow in time for the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Sovereign_States" title="Union of Sovereign States">New Union Treaty</a> signing on 20 August.</p>
<p>On 17 August, the members of the <a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">GKChP</a> met at a <a href="/wiki/KGB" title="KGB">KGB</a> guesthouse in Moscow and studied the treaty document. They believed the pact would pave the way to the Soviet Union's breakup, and decided that it was time to act. The next day, Baklanov, Boldin, Shenin, and USSR Deputy Defense Minister General <a href="/wiki/Valentin_Varennikov" title="Valentin Varennikov">Valentin Varennikov</a> flew to Crimea for a meeting with Gorbachev. They demanded that Gorbachev either declare a state of emergency or resign and name Yanayev as acting president to allow the members of the GKChP "to restore order" in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-NovayaGazeta51_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovayaGazeta51-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NovayaGazeta59_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovayaGazeta59-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kommersant79717_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommersant79717-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gorbachev has always claimed that he refused point blank to accept the ultimatum.<sup id="cite_ref-NovayaGazeta59_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovayaGazeta59-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Varennikov has insisted that Gorbachev said: "Damn you. Do what you want. But report my opinion!"<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> However, those present at the dacha at the time testified that Baklanov, Boldin, Shenin, and Varennikov had been clearly disappointed and nervous after the meeting with Gorbachev.<sup id="cite_ref-NovayaGazeta59_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovayaGazeta59-15">[15]</a></sup> With Gorbachev's refusal, the conspirators ordered that he remain confined to the Foros dacha; at the same time the dacha's communication lines (which were controlled by the KGB) were shut down. Additional KGB security guards were placed at the dacha gates with orders to stop anybody from leaving.</p>
<p>The members of the <a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">GKChP</a> ordered 250,000 pairs of handcuffs from a factory in <a href="/wiki/Pskov" title="Pskov">Pskov</a> to be sent to Moscow<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and 300,000 arrest forms. Kryuchkov doubled the pay of all KGB personnel, called them back from holiday, and placed them on alert. The <a href="/wiki/Lefortovo_Prison" title="Lefortovo Prison">Lefortovo Prison</a> was emptied to receive prisoners.<sup id="cite_ref-Mitrokhin_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mitrokhin-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_August_Coup">The August Coup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The August Coup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The members of the <a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">GKChP</a> met in the <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Kremlin" title="Moscow Kremlin">Kremlin</a> after Baklanov, Boldin, Shenin and Varennikov returned from the Crimea. Yanayev, Pavlov and Baklanov signed the so-called "Declaration of the Soviet Leadership" in which they declared the state of emergency in all of the USSR and announced that the <b>State Committee of the State of Emergency</b> (Государственный Комитет по Чрезвычайному Положению, ГКЧП, or <i>Gosudarstvenniy Komitet po Chrezvichaynomu Polozheniyu</i>, <i>GKChP</i>) had been created "to manage the country and to effectively maintain the regime of the state of emergency". The GKChP included the following members:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gennady_Yanayev" title="Gennady Yanayev">Gennady Yanayev</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Valentin_Pavlov" title="Valentin Pavlov">Valentin Pavlov</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Kryuchkov" title="Vladimir Kryuchkov">Vladimir Kryuchkov</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Yazov" title="Dmitry Yazov">Dmitry Yazov</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Boris_Pugo" title="Boris Pugo">Boris Pugo</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oleg_Baklanov" title="Oleg Baklanov">Oleg Baklanov</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vasily_Starodubtsev" title="Vasily Starodubtsev">Vasily Starodubtsev</a>, chairman of the USSR Peasant Union</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexander_Tizyakov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alexander Tizyakov (page does not exist)">Alexander Tizyakov</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%A2%D0%B8%D0%B7%D1%8F%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%B2,_%D0%90%D0%BB%D0%B5%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%80_%D0%98%D0%B2%D0%B0%D0%BD%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%87" class="extiw" title="ru:Тизяков, Александр Иванович">ru</a>)</span>, president of the Association of the State Enterprises and Objects of Industry, Transport, and Communications of the USSR<sup id="cite_ref-Kommersant79717_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommersant79717-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gkchppost_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gkchppost-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Yanayev signed the decree naming himself as acting USSR president on the pretext of Gorbachev's inability to perform presidential duties due to "illness".<sup id="cite_ref-gkchppost_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gkchppost-20">[20]</a></sup> These eight collectively became known as the "<a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">Gang of Eight</a>".</p>
<p>The GKChP banned all newspapers in Moscow, except for nine Party-controlled newspapers.<sup id="cite_ref-gkchppost_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gkchppost-20">[20]</a></sup> The GKChP also issued a populist declaration which stated that "the honour and dignity of a Soviet man must be restored."<sup id="cite_ref-gkchppost_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gkchppost-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="19_August">19 August</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 19 August">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Boris_Yeltsin_19_August_1991-1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Boris_Yeltsin_19_August_1991-1.jpg/250px-Boris_Yeltsin_19_August_1991-1.jpg" width="250" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Boris_Yeltsin_19_August_1991-1.jpg/375px-Boris_Yeltsin_19_August_1991-1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/71/Boris_Yeltsin_19_August_1991-1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="334" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Boris_Yeltsin_19_August_1991-1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>All of the <a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">State Committee on the State of Emergency</a> (GKChP) documents were broadcast over the state radio and television starting from 7 a.m. The Russian SFSR-controlled <a href="/wiki/Radio_Rossii" title="Radio Rossii">Radio Rossii</a> and <a href="/wiki/All-Russia_State_Television_and_Radio_Broadcasting_Company" title="All-Russia State Television and Radio Broadcasting Company">Televidenie Rossii</a>, plus "<a href="/wiki/Echo_of_Moscow" title="Echo of Moscow">Ekho Moskvy</a>", the only independent political radio station, were cut off the air.<sup id="cite_ref-Kommersant79720_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommersant79720-21">[21]</a></sup> Armour units of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Guards_Tamanskaya_Motor_Rifle_Division" title="2nd Guards Tamanskaya Motor Rifle Division">Tamanskaya Division</a> and the <a href="/wiki/4th_Guards_Kantemirovskaya_Tank_Division" title="4th Guards Kantemirovskaya Tank Division">Kantemirovskaya tank division</a> rolled into Moscow along with <a href="/wiki/Russian_Airborne_Troops" title="Russian Airborne Troops">paratroops</a>. Four <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_People%27s_Deputies_of_Russia" title="Congress of People's Deputies of Russia">Russian SFSR people's deputies</a> (who were considered the most "dangerous") were detained by the KGB at an army base near Moscow.<sup id="cite_ref-91internalkgbreport_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91internalkgbreport-10">[10]</a></sup> The conspirators considered detaining Russian SFSR President <a href="/wiki/Boris_Yeltsin" title="Boris Yeltsin">Boris Yeltsin</a> upon his arrival from a visit to <a href="/wiki/Kazakh_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic">Kazakhstan</a> on 17 August, or after that when he was at his <a href="/wiki/Dacha" title="Dacha">dacha</a> near Moscow, but for some reason they did not do so. The failure to arrest Yeltsin was to prove fatal to their plans.<sup id="cite_ref-91internalkgbreport_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91internalkgbreport-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NovayaGazeta55_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovayaGazeta55-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NovayaGazeta57_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovayaGazeta57-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yeltsin arrived at the <a href="/wiki/White_House_(Moscow)" title="White House (Moscow)">White House</a>, Russia's parliament building, at 9am on 19 August. Together with Russian SFSR <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Russia" title="Prime Minister of Russia">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Silayev" title="Ivan Silayev">Ivan Silayev</a> and <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_Russia" title="Supreme Soviet of Russia">Supreme Soviet</a> Chairman <a href="/wiki/Ruslan_Khasbulatov" title="Ruslan Khasbulatov">Ruslan Khasbulatov</a>, Yeltsin issued a declaration in which it was stated that a reactionary anti-constitutional coup had taken place. The military was urged not to take part in the coup. The declaration called for a <a href="/wiki/General_strike" title="General strike">general strike</a> with the demand to let <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a> address the people.<sup id="cite_ref-russianbook_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-russianbook-24">[24]</a></sup> This declaration was distributed around Moscow in the form of <a href="/wiki/Pamphlet" title="Pamphlet">flyers</a>.</p>
<p>In the afternoon the citizens of Moscow began to gather around the White House and to erect barricades around it.<sup id="cite_ref-russianbook_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-russianbook-24">[24]</a></sup> In response Gennady Yanayev declared the state of emergency in Moscow at 16:00.<sup id="cite_ref-Kommersant79717_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommersant79717-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gkchppost_20-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gkchppost-20">[20]</a></sup> Yanayev declared at the press conference at 17:00 that Gorbachev was "resting". He said: "Over these years he has got very tired and needs some time to get his health back."<sup id="cite_ref-Kommersant79717_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommersant79717-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Meanwhile, Major Evdokimov, chief of staff of a tank battalion of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Guards_Tamanskaya_Motor_Rifle_Division" title="2nd Guards Tamanskaya Motor Rifle Division">Tamanskaya Division</a> guarding the White House, declared his loyalty to the leadership of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Russian SFSR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-russianbook_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-russianbook-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Yeltsin climbed one of the tanks and addressed the crowd. Unexpectedly, this episode was included in the state media's evening news.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20_August">20 August</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 20 August">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1991_coup_attempt4.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/1991_coup_attempt4.jpg/250px-1991_coup_attempt4.jpg" width="250" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/1991_coup_attempt4.jpg/375px-1991_coup_attempt4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/1991_coup_attempt4.jpg/500px-1991_coup_attempt4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="895" data-file-height="571" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1991_coup_attempt4.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tanks in Red Square</div>

<p>At noon, <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Military_District#note-0" title="Moscow Military District">Moscow military district</a> commander General <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Vasilyevich_Kalinin_(born_1937)" title="Nikolai Vasilyevich Kalinin (born 1937)">Kalinin</a>, whom Yanayev appointed as military commandant of Moscow, declared a <a href="/wiki/Curfew" title="Curfew">curfew</a> in Moscow from 23:00 to 5:00, effective from 20 August.<sup id="cite_ref-NovayaGazeta51_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovayaGazeta51-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kommersant79720_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommersant79720-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-russianbook_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-russianbook-24">[24]</a></sup> This was understood as the sign that the attack on the White House was imminent.</p>
<p>The defenders of the White House prepared themselves, most of them being unarmed. Evdokimov's tanks were moved from the White House in the evening.<sup id="cite_ref-Kommersant79717_16-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommersant79717-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Nezavisimoe_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nezavisimoe-27">[27]</a></sup> The makeshift White House defense headquarters was headed by General <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Kobets" title="Konstantin Kobets">Konstantin Kobets</a>, a Russian SFSR <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_People%27s_Deputies_of_Russia" title="Congress of People's Deputies of Russia">people's deputy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Nezavisimoe_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nezavisimoe-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-heroes_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heroes-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the afternoon, Kryuchkov, Yazov and Pugo finally decided to attack the White House. This decision was supported by other GKChP members. Kryuchkov and Yazov's deputies, KGB general Ageyev and Army general Achalov, respectively, planned the assault, codenamed "Operation Grom" (Thunder), which would gather elements of the <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Group" title="Alpha Group">Alpha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vympel" title="Vympel">Vympel</a> elite special force units, with the support of the paratroopers, Moscow <a href="/wiki/OMON" title="OMON">OMON</a>, the Internal Troops of the <a href="/wiki/ODON" title="ODON">Dzerzhinsky division</a>, three tank companies and a helicopter squadron. <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Group" title="Alpha Group">Alpha Group</a> commander General <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Fyodorovich_Karpukhin" title="Viktor Fyodorovich Karpukhin">Viktor Karpukhin</a> and other senior officers of the unit together with Airborne Troops deputy commander Gen. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lebed" title="Alexander Lebed">Alexander Lebed</a> mingled with the crowds near the White House and assessed the possibility of such an operation. After that, Karpukhin and Vympel commander Colonel Beskov tried to convince Ageyev that the operation would result in bloodshed and should be canceled.<sup id="cite_ref-91internalkgbreport_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91internalkgbreport-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NovayaGazeta51_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NovayaGazeta51-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Argumenty_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Argumenty-29">[29]</a></sup> Lebed, with the consent of his immediate superior, <a href="/wiki/Pavel_Grachev" title="Pavel Grachev">Pavel Grachev</a>, returned to the White House and secretly informed the defense headquarters that the attack would begin at 2:00.<sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Argumenty_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Argumenty-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>'s Supreme Council declared Republic of Estonia sovereign and independent at 23.03.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21_August">21 August</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 21 August">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At about 1:00, not far from the <a href="/wiki/White_House_(Moscow)" title="White House (Moscow)">White House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trolleybus" title="Trolleybus">trolleybuses</a> and street cleaning machines barricaded a tunnel against oncoming Taman Guards <a href="/wiki/Infantry_fighting_vehicle" title="Infantry fighting vehicle">infantry fighting vehicles</a> (IFVs). Three men were killed in the incident with several others wounded. Two of the victims were trying to cover an IFV's observation slit. The crowd later burned the IFV, but no soldiers were killed.<sup id="cite_ref-Kommersant79717_16-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommersant79717-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-heroes_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heroes-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Alpha Group and Vympel did not move to the White House as had been planned and Yazov ordered the troops to pull out from Moscow.</p>
<p>The troops began to move from Moscow at 8:00. The GKChP members met in the Defence Ministry and, not knowing what to do, decided to send Kryuchkov, Yazov, Baklanov, Tizyakov, <a href="/wiki/Anatoly_Lukyanov" title="Anatoly Lukyanov">Anatoly Lukyanov</a>, and Deputy <a href="/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="General Secretary of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union">CPSU General Secretary</a> <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Ivashko" title="Vladimir Ivashko">Vladimir Ivashko</a> to the Crimea to meet Gorbachev, who refused to meet them when they arrived. With the dacha's communications to Moscow restored, Gorbachev declared all the GKChP's decisions void and dismissed its members from their state offices. The <a href="/wiki/Procurator_General_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Procurator General of the Soviet Union">USSR General Prosecutors Office</a> started the investigation of the coup.<sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-russianbook_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-russianbook-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>During that period, the Supreme Council <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Republic of Latvia</a> declared its sovereignty officially completed.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline_of_the_Coup">Timeline of the Coup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Timeline of the Coup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Monday_August_19">Monday August 19</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Monday August 19">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>4.00: General <a href="/w/index.php?title=Igor_Maltsev_(General)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Igor Maltsev (General) (page does not exist)">Igor Maltsev</a> orders the <a href="/wiki/Simferopol_International_Airport" title="Simferopol International Airport">Crimean Airport</a> to close. <a href="/wiki/Warship" title="Warship">Warships</a> are anchored near <a href="/wiki/Foros,_Crimea" title="Foros, Crimea">Kap Foros</a>, which is located near the summer villa of president <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vuosi1991_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vuosi1991-32">[32]</a></sup></li>

Gorbachev is deposed under medical reasons and put under <a href="/wiki/House_arrest" title="House arrest">house arrest</a> at his Crimean summer villa.</li>
<li>5.57: A State of Emergency is declared in all of the territory of the Soviet Union. All the power is under the <a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">State Committee on the State of Emergency</a> whose members are Head of <a href="/wiki/KGB" title="KGB">KGB</a> <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Kryuchkov" title="Vladimir Kryuchkov">Vladimir Kryuchkov</a>, Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Valentin_Pavlov" title="Valentin Pavlov">Valentin Pavlov</a> and Minister of Interior <a href="/wiki/Boris_Pugo" title="Boris Pugo">Boris Pugo</a>.</li>
<li>9.00: Tanks are driven near the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic</a>'s <a href="/wiki/White_House_(Moscow)" title="White House (Moscow)">Parliament Building</a> where President <a href="/wiki/Boris_Yeltsin" title="Boris Yeltsin">Boris Yeltsin</a> is located.</li>
<li>10.40: State Committee on the State of Emergency dissolves the <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_press" class="mw-redirect" title="Freedom of press">freedom of press</a>.</li>
<li>10.45: <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic</a> President Boris Yeltsin condemns the coup discourage people to <a href="/wiki/General_strike" title="General strike">general strike</a>.</li>

<li>20.00: State Committee on the State of Emergency warns Boris Yeltsin not to provoke the masses.</li>
<li>21.00: <a href="/wiki/2nd_Guards_Tamanskaya_Motor_Rifle_Division" title="2nd Guards Tamanskaya Motor Rifle Division">Tamanskaya elite force</a> tanks proceeds to support Yeltsin.</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gorbachev and the GKChP delegation flew to Moscow, where <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Kryuchkov" title="Vladimir Kryuchkov">Kryuchkov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Yazov" title="Dmitry Yazov">Yazov</a>, and Tizyakov were arrested upon arrival in the early hours of 22 August. <a href="/wiki/Boris_Pugo" title="Boris Pugo">Pugo</a> committed suicide along with his wife the next day. Pavlov, <a href="/wiki/Vasily_Starodubtsev" title="Vasily Starodubtsev">Vasily Starodubtsev</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Baklanov" title="Oleg Baklanov">Baklanov</a>, Boldin, and Shenin would be in custody within the next 48 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since several heads of the regional <a href="/wiki/Committee" title="Committee">executive committees</a> supported the GKChP, on 21 August the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_Russia" title="Supreme Soviet of Russia">Supreme Soviet of the Russian SFSR</a> adopted Decision No. 1626-1, which authorized Russian President <a href="/wiki/Boris_Yeltsin" title="Boris Yeltsin">Boris Yeltsin</a> to appoint heads of regional administrations, although the Russian constitution did not empower the president with such authority.<sup id="cite_ref-Konsultant_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Konsultant-33">[33]</a></sup> It passed another decision the next day which declared the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Russia" title="Flag of Russia">old imperial colors</a> as Russia's national flag.<sup id="cite_ref-Konsultant_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Konsultant-33">[33]</a></sup> It eventually replaced the Russian SFSR flag two months later.</p>
<p>On the night of 24 August, the <a href="/wiki/Felix_Dzerzhinsky" title="Felix Dzerzhinsky">Felix Dzerzhinsky</a> statue in front of the <a href="/wiki/Lubyanka_Building" title="Lubyanka Building">KGB building at Dzerzhinskiy Square (Lubianka)</a> was dismantled, while thousands of Moscow citizens took part in the funeral of Dmitry Komar, Vladimir Usov and Ilya Krichevsky, the three citizens who died in the tunnel incident. Gorbachev posthumously awarded them with the title of <a href="/wiki/Hero_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Hero of the Soviet Union">Hero of the Soviet Union</a>. Yeltsin asked their relatives to forgive him for not being able to prevent their deaths.<sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="End_of_the_CPSU">End of the CPSU</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: End of the CPSU">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gorbachev resigned as <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Communist Party of the Soviet Union">CPSU</a> general secretary on 24 August.<sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Ivashko" title="Vladimir Ivashko">Vladimir Ivashko</a> replaced him as acting general secretary but resigned on 29 August. Around the same time, Yeltsin decreed the transfer of the CPSU archives to the state archive authorities, as well as nationalizing all CPSU assets in Russia (which included not only the headquarters of party committees but also educational institutions, hotels, etc.).<sup id="cite_ref-Konsultant_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Konsultant-33">[33]</a></sup> Yeltsin decreed the termination and banning of all Party activities in Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-Konsultant_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Konsultant-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union">Dissolution of the Soviet Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Dissolution of the Soviet Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Belavezha_Accords" title="Belavezha Accords">Belavezha Accords</a></div>
<p>On 24 August, Mikhail Gorbachev created the so-called "Committee for the Operational Management of the Soviet Economy" (Комитет по оперативному управлению народным хозяйством СССР), to replace the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Ministers_(Soviet_Union)" title="Cabinet of Ministers (Soviet Union)">USSR Cabinet of Ministers</a> headed by <a href="/wiki/Valentin_Pavlov" title="Valentin Pavlov">Valentin Pavlov</a>, a GKChP member. Russian prime minister <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Silayev" title="Ivan Silayev">Ivan Silayev</a> headed this committee. On the same day the <a href="/wiki/Verkhovna_Rada" title="Verkhovna Rada">Verkhovna Rada</a> adopted the <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_Independence_of_Ukraine" title="Declaration of Independence of Ukraine">Declaration of Independence of Ukraine</a> and called for a referendum on support of the Declaration of Independence. The <a href="/wiki/Byelorussian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic">Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic</a>, the third powerful republic in the union, also declared its independence the next day on 25 August which then established the <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Republic of Belarus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-byind_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-byind-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 5 September, the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_People%27s_Deputies_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Congress of People's Deputies of the Soviet Union">Congress of People's Deputies of the Soviet Union</a> adopted Soviet Law No. 2392-1 "On the Authorities of the Soviet Union in the Transitional Period" under which the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union">Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union</a> had replaced Congress of People's Deputies and was reformed. Two new legislative chambers—the Soviet of the Union (Совет Союза) and the Soviet of Republics (Совет Республик)—replaced the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_of_the_Union" title="Soviet of the Union">Soviet of the Union</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_of_Nationalities" title="Soviet of Nationalities">Soviet of Nationalities</a> (both elected by the USSR Congress of Peoples Deputies). The Soviet of the Union was to be formed by the popularly elected USSR <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_People%27s_Deputies_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Congress of People's Deputies of the Soviet Union">people's deputies</a>. The Soviet of Republics was to include 20 deputies from each union republic plus one deputy to represent each <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_region" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous region">autonomous region</a> of each union republic (both USSR <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_People%27s_Deputies_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Congress of People's Deputies of the Soviet Union">people's deputies</a> and republican <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_People%27s_Deputies_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Congress of People's Deputies (disambiguation)">people's deputies</a>) delegated by the legislatures of the union republic. Russia was an exception with 52 deputies. However, the delegation of each union republic was to have only one vote in the Soviet of Republics. The laws were to be first adopted by the Soviet of the Union and then by the Soviet of Republics.</p>
<p>Also created was the <a href="/wiki/State_Council_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="State Council of the Soviet Union">USSR State Council</a> (Государственный совет СССР), which included <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="President of the Soviet Union">the USSR President</a> and the presidents of union republics. The "Committee for the Operational Management of the Soviet Economy" was replaced by the USSR Inter-republican Economic Committee (Межреспубликанский экономический комитет СССР), also headed by <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Silayev" title="Ivan Silayev">Ivan Silayev</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 August, the first state became independent, <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova" title="Parliament of the Republic of Moldova">Supreme Soviet of Moldova</a> declared the independence of <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldova</a> from the Soviet Union. The Supreme Soviets of Azerbaijan and Kyrgyzstan did the same on 30 and 31 August respectively. Afterwards, on 6 September the newly created Soviet State Council recognized the independence of <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Estonia had declared re-independence on 20 August, Latvia on the following day, while Lithuania had done so already on 11 March 1990. Three days later, on 9 September the <a href="/wiki/Tajik_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Tajik Soviet Socialist Republic">Supreme Soviet of Tajikistan</a> declared the independence of <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a> from the Soviet Union. Further more, in September over 99% percent of voters in <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic">Armenia</a> voted for a referendum approving the Republic's commitment to independence. The immediate aftermath of that vote was the Armenian <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet" title="Supreme Soviet">Supreme Soviet</a>'s declaration of independence, issued on 21 September. By 27 October the <a href="/wiki/Turkmen_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Turkmen Soviet Socialist Republic">Supreme Soviet of Turkmenistan</a> declared the independence of <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a> from the Soviet Union. On the 1 of December Ukraine held a referendum, in which more than 90% of residents supported the <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_Independence_of_Ukraine" title="Declaration of Independence of Ukraine">Act of Independence of Ukraine</a>.</p>
<p>By November, the only Soviet Republics that had not declared independence were Russia, Belarus, Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. That same month, seven republics (Russia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, Turkmenistan, and Tajikistan) agreed to a new union treaty that would form a confederation called the Union of Sovereign States. However, this confederation never materialized.</p>
<p>On 8 December Boris Yeltsin, <a href="/wiki/Leonid_Kravchuk" title="Leonid Kravchuk">Leonid Kravchuk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stanislav_Shushkevich" title="Stanislav Shushkevich">Stanislav Shushkevich</a>—respectively leaders of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> (which adopted that name in August 1991)—as well as the prime ministers of the republics met in <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a>, the capital of Belarus, where they created the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">Commonwealth of Independent States</a> (CIS) and annulled the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_on_the_Creation_of_the_USSR" title="Treaty on the Creation of the USSR">1922 union treaty</a> that had established the Soviet Union. Doubts remained about legitimacy of the signing that took place on 8 December, so another signing ceremony was held in <a href="/wiki/Almaty" title="Almaty">Alma-Ata</a> on 21 December to expand the CIS to include <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> and the five republics of <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> joined in 1993, only to withdraw in 2008 after <a href="/wiki/Russo-Georgian_War" title="Russo-Georgian War">conflict between Georgia and Russia</a>; the three Baltic states never joined.</p>
<p>On 24 December 1991, <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">the Russian Federation</a>, with the concurrence of the other republics of the Commonwealth of Independent States, informed the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> that it would <a href="/wiki/Russia_and_the_United_Nations" title="Russia and the United Nations">succeed the Soviet Union in its membership in the UN</a> and in its seat on the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council" title="United Nations Security Council">United Nations Security Council</a>. No member state of the UN formally objected to this step. The legitimacy of this act has been <a href="/wiki/Russia_and_the_United_Nations#Legality" title="Russia and the United Nations">questioned by some legal scholars</a> as the Soviet Union itself was not constitutionally succeeded by the Russian Federation, but merely dissolved. Others argued that the international community had already established the precedent of recognizing the Soviet Union as the legal successor of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>, and so recognizing <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">the Russian Federation</a> as the Soviet Union's successor state was valid. After Russia's succession, on 25 December 1991, Gorbachev announced his resignation as Soviet president. The red hammer and sickle flag of the Soviet Union was lowered from the Senate building in the Kremlin and replaced with the tricolor flag of Russia. The largest republic, the Russian SFSR was renamed to the Russian Federation. The next day, 26 December 1991, the Soviet Union officially ceased to exist, thus ending the life of the world's first and oldest socialist state. All former Soviet embassies became Russian embassies while Russia received the nuclear weapons from the other former republics. A <a href="/wiki/1993_Russian_constitutional_crisis" title="1993 Russian constitutional crisis">constitutional crisis</a> occurred in 1993 had been escalated into violence and the new <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Russia" title="Constitution of Russia">constitution</a> adopted officially abolished the entire Soviet government.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginning_of_radical_economic_reforms_in_Russia">Beginning of radical economic reforms in Russia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Beginning of radical economic reforms in Russia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Economic_history_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Economic history of the Russian Federation">Economic history of the Russian Federation</a></div>
<p>On 1 November 1991, the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_People%27s_Deputies_of_Russia" title="Congress of People's Deputies of Russia">RSFSR Congress of People's Deputies</a> issued Decision No. 1831-1 On the Legal Support of the Economic Reform whereby the Russian president (<a href="/wiki/Boris_Yeltsin" title="Boris Yeltsin">Boris Yeltsin</a>) was granted the right to issue decrees required for the economic reform even if they contravened the laws. Such decrees entered into force if they were not repealed within 7 days by the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_Russia" title="Supreme Soviet of Russia">Supreme Soviet of the Russian SFSR</a> or its Presidium.<sup id="cite_ref-Konsultant_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Konsultant-33">[33]</a></sup> Five days later, Boris Yeltsin, in addition to the duties of the President, assumed the duties of the prime minister. <a href="/wiki/Yegor_Gaidar" title="Yegor Gaidar">Yegor Gaidar</a> became deputy prime minister and simultaneously economic and finance minister. On November 15, 1991 Boris Yeltsin issued Decree No. 213 On the Liberalization of Foreign Economic Activity on the Territory of the RSFSR whereby all Russian companies were allowed to import and to export goods and to acquire foreign currency (previously all foreign trade had been tightly controlled by the state).<sup id="cite_ref-Konsultant_33-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Konsultant-33">[33]</a></sup> Following the issuing of Decree No. 213, on 3 December 1991 Boris Yeltsin issued Decree No. 297 On the Measures to Liberalize Prices whereby from 2 January 1992 most previously existing <a href="/wiki/Price_controls" title="Price controls">price controls</a> were abolished.<sup id="cite_ref-Konsultant_33-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Konsultant-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trial_of_the_members_of_the_GKChP">Trial of the members of the GKChP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Trial of the members of the GKChP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">GKChP</a> members and their accomplices were charged with <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">treason</a> in the form of a conspiracy aimed at capturing power. However, by the end of 1992 they were all released from custody pending trial. The trial in the Military Chamber of the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Russia" title="Supreme Court of Russia">Russian Supreme Court</a> began on 14 April 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> On 23 February 1994 the <a href="/wiki/State_Duma" title="State Duma">State Duma</a> declared <a href="/wiki/Amnesty" title="Amnesty">amnesty</a> for all GKChP members and their accomplices, along with the participants of the <a href="/wiki/1993_Russian_constitutional_crisis" title="1993 Russian constitutional crisis">October 1993 crisis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Konsultant_33-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Konsultant-33">[33]</a></sup> They all accepted the amnesty, except for General Varennikov, who demanded the continuation of the trial and was finally acquitted on 11 August 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parliamentary_commission">Parliamentary commission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Parliamentary commission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1991 the Parliamentary Commission for Investigating Causes and Reasons of the coup attempt was established under <a href="/wiki/Lev_Ponomaryov" title="Lev Ponomaryov">Lev Ponomaryov</a>, but in 1992 it was dissolved at <a href="/wiki/Ruslan_Khasbulatov" title="Ruslan Khasbulatov">Ruslan Khasbulatov</a>'s insistence.</p>
