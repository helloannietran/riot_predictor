<p>On 29 December 2008, violent <a href="/wiki/Riot" title="Riot">riots</a> first broke out in <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> amid protests against the <a href="/wiki/Gaza_War_(2008%E2%80%9309)" title="Gaza War (2008–09)">Gaza War</a>, starting outside the <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_mission" title="Diplomatic mission">embassy</a>. Riots broke out again following a protest on 4 January 2009, while the most violent and destructive riots took place on 8 and 10 January when riots spread throughout the city with widespread destruction of private and public property, clashes between rioters and police with several injuries, as well as attacks on civilians, including individuals targeted due to being thought by rioters to be <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a>. Around 200 people were arrested in total, mainly <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> youth, supported by left-wing autonomous <a href="/wiki/Blitz_(movement)" title="Blitz (movement)">Blitz</a> activists.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline_of_the_riots">Timeline of the riots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008%E2%80%9309_Oslo_riots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Timeline of the riots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 29 December 2008, around 1,000 anti-Israel protesters moved towards the Israeli embassy in Parkveien, where speeches were held by people including <a href="/wiki/Inga_Marte_Thorkildsen" title="Inga Marte Thorkildsen">Inga Marte Thorkildsen</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Left_Party_(Norway)" title="Socialist Left Party (Norway)">Socialist Left Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-db301208_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-db301208-1">[1]</a></sup> Other organisations behind the demonstration were the <a href="/wiki/Red_Party_(Norway)" title="Red Party (Norway)">Red Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Youth_(Norway)" title="Red Youth (Norway)">Red Youth</a> (RU), <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Youth_(Norway)" title="Socialist Youth (Norway)">Socialist Youth</a> (SU) and the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_People%27s_Aid" title="Norwegian People's Aid">Norwegian People's Aid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The protest turned violent when around 100 youth, mainly Blitz activists and "young boys" breached police roadblocks and began throwing <a href="/wiki/Molotov_cocktail" title="Molotov cocktail">Molotov cocktails</a> and stones at the embassy and the police, striking several police officers, amid rioters heard shouting "<a href="/wiki/Takbir" title="Takbir">allahu akbar</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-db301208_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-db301208-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vg301208_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg301208-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Gasoline was drawn from nearby cars to set fire to trash bins that were launched against the police.<sup id="cite_ref-db301208_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-db301208-1">[1]</a></sup> Three windows of the hair salon of a well-known <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">homosexual</a> was smashed, with circumstances suggesting a <a href="/wiki/Hate_crime" title="Hate crime">hate crime</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-antisemitism_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-antisemitism-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Police responded by firing tear gas at the rioters, and eventually detained nine youths of which four were arrested, including two <a href="/wiki/Asylum_seeker" title="Asylum seeker">asylum seekers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-db301208_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-db301208-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vg301208_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg301208-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 4 January 2009, an anti-Israel demonstration arranged by the Palestine Committee of Norway, Red Party and Blitz began outside the <a href="/wiki/Storting_building" title="Storting building">Norwegian parliament building</a> and then moved to the Israeli embassy. Members of the crowd grew violent, and around 200 protesters including <a href="/wiki/Hezbollah" title="Hezbollah">Hezbollah</a> supporters began throwing stones and shooting fireworks against the police. The rioters were dispersed by police firing tear gas, after receiving several warnings.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 8 January, around 200 police officers were stationed out in anticipation of protests as a peaceful pro-Israel rally arranged by organisations such as <a href="/wiki/With_Israel_for_Peace" title="With Israel for Peace">With Israel for Peace</a> (MIFF) was to be held outside the Norwegian parliament building, with the <a href="/wiki/Progress_Party_(Norway)" title="Progress Party (Norway)">Progress Party</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Siv_Jensen" title="Siv Jensen">Siv Jensen</a> scheduled to give a pro-Israel speech.<sup id="cite_ref-vg080109_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg080109-9">[9]</a></sup> During Jensen's speech, anti-Israel activists started throwing rocks at the pro-Israel demonstrators, forcing Jensen to leave the podium.<sup id="cite_ref-vg080109b_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg080109b-10">[10]</a></sup> The police used tear gas when rioters attacked a bus that tried to evacuate pro-Israeli activists from the area, which included a large number of elderly demonstrators.<sup id="cite_ref-vg080109b_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg080109b-10">[10]</a></sup> A pro-Israel protester was attacked and injured by anti-Israel protesters shouting "take him, he's a Jew", "fucking Jew" and "allahu akbar".<sup id="cite_ref-dagbladet_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dagbladet-11">[11]</a></sup> Among other slogans, protesters shouted "death to the Jews," "kill the Jews" and "slaughter the Jews" in <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-antisemitism_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-antisemitism-5">[5]</a></sup> An additional fifteen police officers from the Asker and Bærum police district were eventually brought in for assistance.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The final count reported forty shop windows to have been smashed in the riots, and several cars and buses damaged, including fifteen police cars.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> At least six people were reported to have been injured, of which five police officers, one mutilated in the face by an iron rod.<sup id="cite_ref-nrk090109_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrk090109-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Police said they had found several secret stashes of Molotov cocktails, club weapons and knives throughout Oslo during the evening.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> 37 mainly immigrant-background rioters were detained by the police,<sup id="cite_ref-nrk090109_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrk090109-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> of which fifteen were brought into custody, and nine charged with violence against police.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 9 and 10 January new anti-Israel demonstrations were arranged by several organisations, including the <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_Youth_League_(Norway)" title="Workers' Youth League (Norway)">Workers' Youth League</a> (AUF), Red Party, Red Youth (RU), Socialist Youth (SU), Norwegian People's Aid, <a href="/wiki/Rabita_Mosque" title="Rabita Mosque">Islamic Association</a>, and other pro-Palestine and Muslim organisations.<sup id="cite_ref-nrk090109_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrk090109-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Blitz stated openly that they supported the violent riots.<sup id="cite_ref-nrk090109_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrk090109-14">[14]</a></sup> On 10 January, 3,000 demonstrators were joined by the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Confederation_of_Trade_Unions" title="Norwegian Confederation of Trade Unions">Norwegian Confederation of Trade Unions</a> (LO) and Minister of Finance <a href="/wiki/Kristin_Halvorsen" title="Kristin Halvorsen">Kristin Halvorsen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nrk100109_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrk100109-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The protest soon erupted into new riots as fireworks and rocks were thrown at the police and the Israeli embassy, with at least two people injured and several police officers struck by objects.<sup id="cite_ref-nrk100109_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrk100109-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The violence spread throughout Oslo, and numerous shop windows were smashed and cars damaged.<sup id="cite_ref-vg110109_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg110109-23">[23]</a></sup> Five <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a> restaurants were destroyed in the riots because of a false rumor spread by text message that all the money McDonald's earned that day would go to support Israel.<sup id="cite_ref-vg110109_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg110109-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Some of the youngest rioters reported to have been told by older youths to "hunt for Jews", with one group severely beating up a shop owner accused of being a Jew.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Order_of_Freemasons" title="Norwegian Order of Freemasons">Oslo Freemasonry Lodge</a>, which hosted a children's Christmas party with 300 people was deliberately attacked with fireworks after crushing a window open, nearly causing a fire.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The police detained 160 rioters during the evening, charging eleven with property damage and violence against police.<sup id="cite_ref-vg110109_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg110109-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>A total of 194 protesters were arrested during the 8 and 10 January riots.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The police stated that they would investigate all the arrested and that most of them would receive fines of 9,000 <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_krone" title="Norwegian krone">NOK</a> (around 1,300 USD).<sup id="cite_ref-dagbladet_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dagbladet-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vg110109_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg110109-23">[23]</a></sup> In the end, only ten rioters were prosecuted by the police,<sup id="cite_ref-vg250309_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg250309-29">[29]</a></sup> and less than ten convicted.<sup id="cite_ref-forskning_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forskning-30">[30]</a></sup> The Oslo Trade Association called the small number of prosecutions by the police "unacceptable" and "deeply worrying".<sup id="cite_ref-vg250309_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vg250309-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Analysis_and_aftermath">Analysis and aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008%E2%80%9309_Oslo_riots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Analysis and aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In his book <i>The Anti-Jewish Riots in Oslo</i> (2010), Norwegian author and editor Eirik Eiglad, himself a socialist who was present in Oslo during the riots, wrote:<sup id="cite_ref-antisemitism_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-antisemitism-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>As far as I can judge, these were the largest anti-Jewish riots in Norwegian history. Even before and during World War II, when anti-Semitic prejudices were strong, public policies were discriminatory, and the Nazified State Police efficiently confiscated Jewish property and deported Jews on that despicable slave ship SS <i>Donau</i> - even then, Norway had not seen anti-Jewish outbursts of this scale. This country had no previous history of wanton anti-Jewish mass violence.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Police investigators noted similarities in the <i>modus operandi</i> of the Oslo riots with earlier riots <a href="/wiki/2005_French_riots" title="2005 French riots">in Paris</a> and in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In cooperation with Norwegian education authorities, Islamic leaders in Norway initiated "dialogue meetings" with youths in <a href="/wiki/Mosque" title="Mosque">mosques</a> following the riots, with the aim of "using the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>" to reach out to youths who had participated in the riots.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tariq_Ramadan" title="Tariq Ramadan">Tariq Ramadan</a> later visited Oslo and held speeches in the <a href="/wiki/Rabita_Mosque" title="Rabita Mosque">Rabita Mosque</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-forskning_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forskning-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>The riots have been credited by <a href="/wiki/Sociology" title="Sociology">sociologists</a> for "awakening" young Norwegian Muslims politically.<sup id="cite_ref-forskning_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forskning-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-journal_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journal-33">[33]</a></sup> Others have drawn connections to February 2010, when thousands of Oslo taxi drivers blocked the city centre, and 3,000 Muslims took part in an illegal demonstration against newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Dagbladet" title="Dagbladet">Dagbladet</a></i> for publishing a Muhammad cartoon in the context of a news story about an internet link (which the newspaper strongly criticised), during which one of the speakers, <a href="/wiki/Mohyeldeen_Mohammad" title="Mohyeldeen Mohammad">Mohyeldeen Mohammad</a> "warned" of a 9/11 or 7/7 in Norway.<sup id="cite_ref-antisemitism_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-antisemitism-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VG2_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VG2-34">[34]</a></sup> The riots have later been noted as an important shared experience and common denominator for many members of <a href="/wiki/Profetens_Ummah" title="Profetens Ummah">Profetens Ummah</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Foreign_rebel_fighters_in_the_Syrian_Civil_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Foreign rebel fighters in the Syrian Civil War">Norwegian jihadists</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Civil_War" title="Syrian Civil War">Syrian Civil War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
