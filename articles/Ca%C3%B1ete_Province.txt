<p><b>Cañete</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">province</a> located in southern <a href="/wiki/Lima_Region" title="Lima Region">Lima Region</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. It is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Lima_Province" title="Lima Province">Lima Province</a> on the north, the <a href="/wiki/Ica_Region" title="Ica Region">Ica Region</a> on the south, the <a href="/wiki/Huarochir%C3%AD_Province" title="Huarochirí Province">Huarochirí</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yauyos_Province" title="Yauyos Province">Yauyos</a> provinces on the east, and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> on the west. Its capital is the town of <a href="/wiki/San_Vicente_de_Ca%C3%B1ete_District" title="San Vicente de Cañete District">San Vicente de Cañete District</a>. San Luis is the Capital of the <a href="/wiki/Afro-Peruvian" title="Afro-Peruvian">Afro-Peruvian</a> <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">Folklore</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ca%C3%B1ete_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The weather of the province's coastal area has two well-defined seasons:</p>
<ul>
<li>The sunny season, with temperatures of around 30&#160;°C (86.4&#160;°F), during daytime.</li>
<li>The "sunless" season (May–December), during which the sun is covered by clouds. The extreme <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">humidity</a> causes a sensation of cold during this season, even if the temperature never gets lower than 11&#160;°C (51.8&#160;°F).</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attractions">Attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ca%C3%B1ete_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ca%C3%B1ete_River_May_2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/ab/Ca%C3%B1ete_River_May_2012.jpg/220px-Ca%C3%B1ete_River_May_2012.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/ab/Ca%C3%B1ete_River_May_2012.jpg/330px-Ca%C3%B1ete_River_May_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/ab/Ca%C3%B1ete_River_May_2012.jpg/440px-Ca%C3%B1ete_River_May_2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ca%C3%B1ete_River_May_2012.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Cañete River</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The coast of Cañete, with beaches such as Asia, Chilca, Puerto Viejo, León Dormido,Costa del Campo, Totoritas, Chocaya, Chepeconde, Puerto Fiel, Gallardo and Cerro Azul, is one of the province's main touristic attractions. The pleasant valley of <a href="/wiki/Lunahuan%C3%A1" title="Lunahuaná">Lunahuaná</a>, a paradise for adventure sports lovers, is just half an hour from San Vicente de Cañete. The main attraction is the fast running <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ca%C3%B1ete_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cañete River (page does not exist)">Cañete River</a>, which features <a href="/wiki/Rapids" title="Rapids">rapids</a> up to Class IV. Each year, the valley hosts a festival involving rafting, trekking, gliding, mountain biking and fishing competitions. There are a few annual festivals in Cañete. The most important ones are Festival of <a href="/wiki/Musica_negra" class="mw-redirect" title="Musica negra">Black Art</a> in late August and Adventure Sports Festival in Lunahuaná, held in February.</p>
