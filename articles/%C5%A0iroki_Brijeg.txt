<p><b>Široki Brijeg</b> (<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Hr-%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b0/Hr-%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg.ogg" class="internal" title="Hr-Široki Brijeg.ogg">pronunciation</a></span>&#160;<small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Hr-%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg.ogg" title="File:Hr-Široki Brijeg.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>. It is located in the <a href="/wiki/West_Herzegovina_Canton" title="West Herzegovina Canton">West Herzegovina Canton</a>, a unit of the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>. It is one of the twelve official cities of <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name of the city means "wide hill". It is sometimes referred to as Široki Brig or simply Široki ("wide"). Between 1945 and 1990, the town was officially called Lištica, after the river that flows through it.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The river <a href="/wiki/Li%C5%A1tica_river" class="mw-redirect" title="Lištica river">Lištica</a> runs through Široki Brijeg. The town is 20–25&#160;km (12–16&#160;mi) from <a href="/wiki/Mostar" title="Mostar">Mostar</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-michelin1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-michelin1-2">[2]</a></sup> 42&#160;km (26&#160;mi) from <a href="/wiki/Me%C4%91ugorje" class="mw-redirect" title="Međugorje">Međugorje</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and 88&#160;km (55&#160;mi) from the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic" class="mw-redirect" title="Adriatic">Adriatic</a> coast (<a href="/wiki/Plo%C4%8De" title="Ploče">Ploče</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Široki Brijeg Municipality today numbers around 30,000 people, and the city itself about 13,000. The area of the municipality amounts to 1,168&#160;ha (2,886 acres; 5&#160;sq&#160;mi). The center of town is at 270&#160;m (886&#160;ft) above sea level, while its area is known as "lower Herzegovina". Almost the entire northern part of the municipality itself, however, belongs to "high Herzegovina", the highest point being the <i>Bile stine</i> ("White Rocks") near <a href="/wiki/Donji_Crna%C4%8D" title="Donji Crnač">Donji Crnač</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Široki Brijeg has mild submediterranean-mountain climate. Winters are chilly and often very cold. Summers are warm.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terrain">Terrain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Terrain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The wider area of the municipality of Široki Brijeg is located on the characteristic, complex structural forms known as the "high <a href="/wiki/Karst" title="Karst">karst</a> zone" in geologic terms. The basic characteristic is the rocky limestone relief in various karst forms (sinks, caves, sinkholes, karst fields, etc.), as well as deeply cut valleys with occasional torrents flows. The Široki Brijeg area is located on the direction of tectonic units Rakitno-Hrgud and spreads over Rakitno, Vardi mountains, Mostarsko Blato, Rotimlje and Hrgud to <a href="/wiki/Trebi%C5%A1njica" title="Trebišnjica">Trebišnjica</a> in the southeast. The area is made-up of up late <a href="/wiki/Jurassic" title="Jurassic">Jurassic</a> sediments, followed by <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paleogene" title="Paleogene">Paleogene</a> age layers, then <a href="/wiki/Neogene" title="Neogene">Neogene</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quaternary" title="Quaternary">Quaternary</a> deposits. Highlights are <a href="/wiki/Early_Cretaceous" title="Early Cretaceous">early Cretaceous</a> dun, well stratified limestone and dolomites. Late <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> deposits, cenoman-turon, are represented by white and pink, massive limestone with the shoot. In Paleogenic layers function Liburnijska and alveolinic-<a href="/wiki/Nummulitic" class="mw-redirect" title="Nummulitic">nummulitic</a> limestones, then <a href="/wiki/Eocenic" class="mw-redirect" title="Eocenic">eocenic</a> flysch, which is represented by marl, sandstone, calcarenite and conglomerates.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Neogene" title="Neogene">Neogenic</a> layers are built of marl, sandy clay, <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> and conglomerates, and can be found in the vicinity of Grabovo Drage and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mostarsko_Blato&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mostarsko Blato (page does not exist)">Mostarsko Blato</a>. <a href="/wiki/Quaternary" title="Quaternary">Quaternary</a> banks of pebbles, sand and water karst saturated found on almost all rocky fields and drifts along the river beds. Between the lower mountain Vardi, Gvozd, Rujan and Trtle (altitude 600-900m) is a karstic depression Kočerinsko, Trnsko, Mokarsko and Ruževo field and Mostar Blato (altitude of 220-300m). Široki Brijeg area belongs to typical <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a> courts Lokve Black - Kidačke Njive, Resnica - deposit and Uzarići - Knešpolje. This area was devastated shoot landfills and tailings, and rehabilitation requirements.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Waters">Waters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Waters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The water from the municipality of Široki Brijeg belong to the basin of the Neretva river. The main surface currents towards Mostar Blato the ticket with Ugrovača, Mokašnica, Crnašnica and Žvatić. The river belongs Listica of surface waters Čabulje, who bujični aquifer Brinje collected from Ladin and Dobrinja. Brinje whose rudiments north of Bogodola, below kote Kulic (1199) runs to the west and on their way to Listica receives at Prskalo stream Ladin, and 2.5 kilometers below, the stream of water Dobrinski. These streams buy all surface waters during the period of abundant precipitation and dissolution of snow on the south-western parts of the mountain Čabulje.</p>
<p>Surface waters come to <a href="/wiki/Rakitno_field" title="Rakitno field">Rakitno field</a> are being dried by water points, Jelica, Zmijinac and during major precipitation formed a periodic watercourse <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ugrova%C4%8Da&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ugrovača (page does not exist)">Ugrovača</a> that going deeply carved canyon Brin, receiving side stream, and in the village of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trn,_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Trn, Bosnia and Herzegovina (page does not exist)">Trn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ko%C4%8Derin" title="Kočerin">Kočerin</a> water fields, and on the road to Blato Mostarskog, in Siroki Brijeg center connects with the <a href="/wiki/River_Li%C5%A1tica" class="mw-redirect" title="River Lištica">river Lištica</a>.</p>

<p>After the Turkish conquest in the middle of 15th century the population was mainly rural and in the mountains. Until the middle of 19th century, Herzegovina was cut off from social, cultural and political events in other European countries.</p>

<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> the <a href="/wiki/Usta%C5%A1e" title="Ustaše">Ustaše</a> held the city together with <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy_(1861%E2%80%931946)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Italy (1861–1946)">Italian</a> troops. During their presence, the <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italians</a> built several <a href="/wiki/Fort" class="mw-redirect" title="Fort">forts</a> for observation of the city<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>Like some cities in West <a href="/wiki/Herzegovina" title="Herzegovina">Herzegovina</a>, Široki Brijeg was labeled as a pro-Ustaše and a pro-fascisct region<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. It was therefore demonized by the officials of <a href="/wiki/SFRY" class="mw-redirect" title="SFRY">Yugoslavia</a> and was renamed <i>Lištica</i> (by the name of the river) instead of Široki Brijeg (<i>Wide Hill</i>). Investment in the city was also very poor. A number of people emigrated to <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">Dalmatia</a>, as well as to <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> as <a href="/wiki/Gastarbeiter" title="Gastarbeiter">gastarbeiters</a>.</p>
<p>In 1985, the local <a href="/wiki/League_of_Communists_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="League of Communists of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Communist Party</a> built a monument to the <a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_Partisans" title="Yugoslav Partisans">Yugoslav Partisans</a> in the centre of the town with Partisans ostensibly reburied beneath it. On 3 May 2013 the monument was removed to explore the mass grave, revealing the remains of 95 people.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEABCPortal4_May_2013_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEABCPortal4_May_2013-5">[5]</a></sup> The remains were characteristic of those killed by the Partisans themselves, with bodies being tied up with wire and some German dog tags being recovered.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHRSvijet6_May_2013_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHRSvijet6_May_2013-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Agricultural production in the municipality of Široki Brijeg is mostly reduced to the production for their own use on yards and a minor part of the arable land. It is the production of agricultural products for the market is very small compared to the alternatives, and refers primarily to the production of wine, a little bit in the production of vegetables and flowers, very little in the livestock and milk production.</p>
<p>This resulted in a large percentage of agricultural land is not used- according to latest statistics - even 60% that could be used for agricultural land is not processed.</p>
<p>The lack of clear government strategy and orientation towards the launching and development of agricultural production is also very important feature of the present situation in agriculture .</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Prior_to_the_war">Prior to the war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Prior to the war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Unlike twenty years ago, is now in agricultural production there is no fundamental cultural production, production that would be meaningful and possible in a larger area of the municipality. For many years it was tobacco. Together with the war has fallen down and the existing state system and thus the former system of organization of agricultural production. In the entire period since then until today, the new system was established.</p>
<p>It is the whole country, especially the area where the municipality brought nekontroliranom import all kinds of things. So the majority of agricultural products has become cheaper to import than produce.</p>
<p>The result has already been mentioned drastically reducing obrađivanih area, reducing the number of cattle and even up to five times, the entire reduction in volume of agricultural production. Finally it came to life the feeling that it is impossible to engage in agricultural production as the production activity of which can be live or something and make money.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_war">After the war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: After the war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The municipality's overall economic performance is stronger than that of the surrounding municipalities - primarily in the Entrepreneurial Sector, and the time orientation and working-age population by non-sector higher. Therefore, the percentage of uncultivated land is the largest in the municipality in comparison to the surrounding ones- 60% (Posušje 59%, 49% Ljubuski and Grude 47%). The number of residents who are engaged solely in agriculture is relatively smallest in Siroki Brijeg.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the area of industrial production in the municipality are significantly represented meat industry and metal industry, and to a smaller production of footwear, manufacture of construction products and stone processing, production of insulation materials, graphic activity, etc.</p>
<p>Official statistics, which regularly publishes the Federal Bureau of Statistics tracked the movement of industrial production at the level of the Federation and cantons, so that these (official) data for the municipality does not have. However, the basic features of industrial production in this region in the West would be essentially similar and reflect the movement of industrial production for the municipality of Siroki Brijeg.</p>
<p>In the period 2004./2005. The recorded slight growth of the index of industrial production (101.3). When the production of metals and production of metal products recorded a significant growth (index 2005./2004. = 134.3 and 180.4), production of paper products (index = 110.7), while other areas of industrial production recorded a significant decrease ( such as food and beverages: Index = 80.0, mining: index = 71.4).</p>
<p>For further growth of industrial production are essential investments in the development of existing industrial capacity in the areas that are currently represented in the municipality and which employ a large number of workers. This primarily refers to the production of metals and metal products. These activities according to official statistics reported a continued growth during 2005. year. The establishment and construction of new or expansion of existing business and industrial zones is prerequisite for the growth of manufacturing sector, attract domestic and foreign capital and create new jobs.</p>

Basketball Club, founded in 1974., Still bears the name of <a href="/wiki/HKK_%C5%A0iroki" title="HKK Široki">HKK Široki</a> Eronet HT and the multiple champion, as well as won multiple State Cup and most postwar BiH club titles with 9 National Championships and 9 titles Cup conquerors of Bosnia and Herzegovina.</p>

<p>The town is the birthplace of the football player of the year in Austria <a href="/wiki/Mario_Bazina" title="Mario Bazina">Mario Bazina</a>, as well as Stanko Bubalo and popular Croatian singer <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Mikulic" class="mw-redirect" title="Ivan Mikulic">Ivan Mikulic</a> who appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a> representing Croatia in 2004. The town is also the birthplace of former Croatian defence minister <a href="/wiki/Gojko_%C5%A0u%C5%A1ak" title="Gojko Šušak">Gojko Šušak</a>, a naturalised Canadian citizen, and the native town/municipality of a number of Croatian politicians. The town holds a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gojko_%C5%A0u%C5%A1ak_Memorial&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gojko Šušak Memorial (page does not exist)">memorial football tournament in Gojko Šušak's honour</a> each year. The town also hosts the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dani_Filma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dani Filma (page does not exist)">Dani Filma</a> (Days of Film) film festival and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Herzegovina_Fest&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="West Herzegovina Fest (page does not exist)">West Herzegovina Fest</a> (amateur art festival).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1971">1971</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: 1971">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>27,285 total</p>
<ul>
<li>Croats - 26.940 (98.73%)</li>
<li>Serbs - 234 (0.85%)</li>
<li>Muslims - 43 (0.15%)</li>
<li>Yugoslavs - 12 (0.04%)</li>
<li>Others - 56 (0.20%)</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1991">1991</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%A0iroki_Brijeg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: 1991">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 1991 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>, the municipality of Široki Brijeg had 26,437 inhabitants:</p>
<ul>
<li>26,231 <a href="/wiki/Croats" title="Croats">Croats</a> (99.2%),</li>
<li>147 <a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbs</a> (0.6%),</li>
<li>19 <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavs" title="Yugoslavs">Yugoslavs</a> (0.07%),</li>
<li>9 <a href="/wiki/Bosniaks" title="Bosniaks">Bosniaks</a> (0.03%),</li>
<li>31 others (0.1%).</li>
</ul>
<p>The town itself had 6,864 inhabitants, 99.37% being Croats.</p>
