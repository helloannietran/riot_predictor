<p>The <b>Bolivarian State of Mérida</b><sup id="cite_ref-edobolivariano1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-edobolivariano1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-edobolivariano2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-edobolivariano2-2">[2]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Estado Bolivariano de Mérida</i></span>), commonly known simply as <b>Mérida State</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es"><i>Estado de Mérida</i></span>, <small>IPA:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[esˈtaðo ðe ˈmeɾiða]</a></span>) is one of the 23 <a href="/wiki/States_of_Venezuela" title="States of Venezuela">states</a> of <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. The state capital is <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9rida,_M%C3%A9rida" title="Mérida, Mérida">Mérida</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Libertador_Municipality,_M%C3%A9rida" title="Libertador Municipality, Mérida">Libertador Municipality</a>.</p>
<p>Located in the Western <a href="/wiki/Andean_Region,_Venezuela" title="Andean Region, Venezuela">Andean Region</a>, Mérida State covers a total surface area of 11,300 square kilometres (4,363&#160;sq&#160;mi), making it the fifteenth-largest in Venezuela. In 2011, had a census population of 828,592, the fourteenth most populous.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_and_Vegetation">Climate and Vegetation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A9rida_(state)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Climate and Vegetation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The climate is temperate but in the relatively small area of Mérida state, there are usually several dramatic climatic changes that occur daily. The climate in Mérida city has an average high temperatures between 24&#160;°C and 25&#160;°C, and low temperatures between 14&#160;°C and 16&#160;°C. At higher peaks on the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Nevada_de_M%C3%A9rida" title="Sierra Nevada de Mérida">Sierra Nevada de Mérida</a>, temperatures tend to be under 0&#160;°C. El Vigía sees temperatures around 28&#160;°C. It rains almost every late afternoon in January–February. These are the coldest months. In August and September it often rains at night. The <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">vegetation</a> is exuberant and there are many <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">rivers</a>, a great number of which are well stocked with <a href="/wiki/Trout" title="Trout">trout</a>, one of the delicacies of Mérida. The most important river in the state is the "Chama" because a large part of the water resources of Venezuela originates in the Mérida mountains. The state tree is the "Bucare" and the state flower is the "Frailejón", The "Coloradito" contains the tree that is found at the highest altitude in the world. In this <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a> <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> the "Oso Frontino" bear and the Condor flies through the skies of the páramos and in the lagoons there are many trout.</p>
