<p><b>Papua Province</b> (<a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a>: <span lang="id" xml:lang="id"><i>Provinsi Papua</i></span>) is the largest and easternmost <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Indonesia" title="Provinces of Indonesia">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. It lies in <a href="/wiki/West_Papua_(region)" class="mw-redirect" title="West Papua (region)">West Papua region</a>, which comprises the Indonesian western half of the island of <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a> and nearby islands. Papua is bordered by the nation of <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a> to the east, and by <a href="/wiki/West_Papua_(province)" title="West Papua (province)">West Papua province</a> to the west. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Jayapura" title="Jayapura">Jayapura</a>. It was formerly called <b>Irian Jaya</b> (before that <b>West Irian</b> or <b>Irian Barat</b>) and comprised all of Indonesian New Guinea. In 2002 the current name was adopted and in 2003 West Papua province was created within West Papua region from western parts of Papua province.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papua_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Papua" is the official Indonesian and internationally recognised name for the province.</p>
<p>During the Dutch colonial era the region was known as part of "Dutch New Guinea" or "<a href="/wiki/Netherlands_New_Guinea" title="Netherlands New Guinea">Netherlands New Guinea</a>". Since its annexation in 1969, it became known as "West Irian" or "Irian Barat" until 1973, and thereafter renamed "Irian Jaya" (roughly translated, "Glorious Irian") by the <a href="/wiki/Suharto" title="Suharto">Suharto</a> administration.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> This was the official name until the name "Papua" was adopted in 2002. Today, the indigenous inhabitants of this province prefer to call themselves Papuans.</p>
<p>The name "West Papua" was adopted in 1961 by the <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea_Council" title="New Guinea Council">New Guinea Council</a> until the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Temporary_Executive_Authority" title="United Nations Temporary Executive Authority">United Nations Temporary Executive Authority</a> (UNTEA) transferred administration to the Republic of Indonesia in 1963. "West Papua" has since been used by Papuans as a self-identifying term, especially by those demanding self-determination, and usually refers to the whole of the Indonesian portion of New Guinea. The other Indonesian province that shares New Guinea, West Irian Jaya, has been officially renamed as <a href="/wiki/West_Papua_(province)" title="West Papua (province)"><i>West Papua</i></a>, or <i>Papua Barat</i>.</p>
<p>Within Indonesia and West Papua itself, 'Papua' usually refers to the entire western half of New Guinea despite its division into separate provinces.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Western New Guinea is generally referred to as 'West Papua' internationally – especially among networks of international solidarity with the West Papuan independence movement.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papua_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GubPapuaTengah.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/GubPapuaTengah.jpg/170px-GubPapuaTengah.jpg" width="170" height="226" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/GubPapuaTengah.jpg/255px-GubPapuaTengah.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/GubPapuaTengah.jpg/340px-GubPapuaTengah.jpg 2x" data-file-width="655" data-file-height="870" /></a>

<p>The province of Papua is governed by a directly elected governor (currently Lukas Enembe) and a regional legislature, DPRP (Dewan Perwakilan Rakyat Papua).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A government organisation that only exists in Papua is the MRP (Majelis Rakyat Papua / Papuan People's Council), which was formed by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Indonesia" title="Government of Indonesia">Indonesian Government</a> in 2005 as a coalition of Papuan tribal chiefs, tasked with arbitration and speaking on behalf of Papuan tribal customs.</p>
<p>Indonesian sovereignty over Papua dates back to 1969, when Indonesia conducted a referendum (referred to by the Indonesian government as the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Free_Choice" title="Act of Free Choice">Act of Free Choice</a>) on the self-determination of the peoples of Papua under an agreement with the United Nations to respect any result. Instead of conducting a democratic referendum amongst the general population, Indonesian security forces forcibly coerced a small number of tribal elders to vote to join Indonesia; some elders were not even made aware that a referendum was to be conducted beforehand. Nevertheless, the agreement with the UN was nominally upheld, and was recognised by the international community in spite of protests. This intensified the <a href="/wiki/Free_Papua_Movement" title="Free Papua Movement">independence movement among indigenous West Papuans</a>, deepening the <a href="/wiki/Papua_conflict" title="Papua conflict">Papua conflict</a>, which began when the Dutch withdrew from the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">East Indies</a> in 1963. The conflict has continued to the present, with Indonesian security forces being accused of numerous human rights abuses in their suppression of the independence movement. The Indonesian government maintains tight control over the region, barring foreign journalists or rights monitors from entering; those who do must do so covertly.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1999 it was proposed to split the province into three government-controlled sectors, sparking Papuan protests.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In January 2003 President <a href="/wiki/Megawati_Sukarnoputri" title="Megawati Sukarnoputri">Megawati Sukarnoputri</a> signed an order dividing Papua into three provinces: Central Irian Jaya (Irian Jaya Tengah), Papua (or East Irian Jaya, Irian Jaya Timur), and <a href="/wiki/West_Papua_(province)" title="West Papua (province)">West Papua</a> (Irian Jaya Barat). The formality of installing a local government for Jaraka in Irian Jaya Barat (West) took place in February 2003 and a governor was appointed in November; a government for Irian Jaya Tengah (Central Irian Jaya) was delayed from August 2003 due to violent local protests. The creation of this separate Central Irian Jaya Province was blocked by Indonesian courts, who declared it to be unconstitutional and in contravention of the Papua's special autonomy agreement. The previous division into two provinces was allowed to stand as an established fact.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papua_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2010 (following the separation of West Papua Province in 2003), the residual Papua Province consisted of 28 <a href="/wiki/Regencies_of_Indonesia" class="mw-redirect" title="Regencies of Indonesia">regencies</a> (<i>kabupaten</i>) and one autonomous city (<i>kota</i>); these regencies and the city are together subdivided into 385 <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Indonesia" class="mw-redirect" title="Districts of Indonesia">districts</a> (<i>kecamatan</i>), and thence into "villages" (<i>kelurahan</i> and <i>desa</i>). In Papua, as well as in the province of West Papua, <i>kelurahan</i> are commonly referred to as <i>distrik</i>.</p>

<p>In 2000 the present area of Papua Province consisted of nine regencies: Biak Numfor, Jayapura, Jayawijaya, Merauke, Mimika, Nabire, Paniai, Puncak Jaya, and Yapen Waropen. On 12 November 2002, Keerom and Sarmi Regencies were split from Jayapura Regency; Bintang Mountains (Pegunungan Bintan), Tolikara and Yahukimo Regencies were split from Jayawijaya Regency; Asmat, Boven Digoel and Mappi Regencies were split from Merauke Regency; and Yapen Waropen Regency was split into <a href="/wiki/Yapen_Islands_Regency" title="Yapen Islands Regency">Yapen Islands Regency</a> (Kepulauan Yapen) and <a href="/wiki/Waropen_Regency" title="Waropen Regency">Waropen Regency</a>. Supiori Regency was split from Biak Numfor Regency on 8 January 2004.</p>
<p>Mamberamo Raya Regency was formed from parts of <a href="/wiki/Sarmi_Regency" title="Sarmi Regency">Sarmi Regency</a> and <a href="/wiki/Waropen_Regency" title="Waropen Regency">Waropen Regency</a> on 15 March 2007 under Law No. 19/2007. The Dogiyai Regency was similarly formed from the southern districts of <a href="/wiki/Nabire_Regency" title="Nabire Regency">Nabire Regency</a> in 2007, and five other new regencies were created on 4 January 2008 by Home Affairs Minister Mardiyanto who also installed five temporary regents. These five new regencies were:</p>
<ul>
<li>4 formerly from part of <a href="/wiki/Jayawijaya_Regency" title="Jayawijaya Regency">Jayawijaya Regency</a>:
<ul>
<li>Central Mamberamo Regency with five districts, with Kobakma as the regental seat. Created by the Law No. 3/2008, the first regent was David Pagawak.</li>
<li>Yalimo Regency also has five districts, with Elelim as the regental capital. Created by the Law No. 4/2008, the first regent was Elia Ibrahim Loupatty.</li>
<li>Lanny Jaya Regency was created by the Law No.5/2008 and has 10 districts. Its first regent was Pribadi Sukartono. The regental capital is Tiom.</li>
<li>Law No. 6/2008 creates Nduga Regency with eight districts and Kenyam as the administrative center. Hans Dortheus was the first regent.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>1 formerly from part of <a href="/wiki/Puncak_Jaya_Regency" title="Puncak Jaya Regency">Puncak Jaya Regency</a>:
<ul>
<li>Puncak Regency also has eight districts with Ilaga as the regental seat being created by the Law No. 7/2008. The first regent was Simon Alom.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Intan Jaya Regency and Deiyai Regency were formed during 2008 from parts of <a href="/wiki/Paniai_Regency" title="Paniai Regency">Paniai Regency</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_new_regencies.2C_cities_and_provinces">Proposed new regencies, cities and provinces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papua_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Proposed new regencies, cities and provinces">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 October 2013 the Indonesian House of Representatives (DPR) began reviewing draft laws on the establishment of 57 prospective regencies/cities (and 8 new provinces).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> This included two new provinces to be formed from parts of the existing Papua Province (and one new province from West Papua Province), as well as the creation of seventeen new regencies and two new cities (independent municipalities). The new regencies will be Gili Menawa, Moyo, Balin Senter, Bogaga, Puncak Trikora, Muara Digul, Admi Korbay, Katengban, Okika, Northwest Yapen, East Yapen, Numfor Island, Yalimek, Mambera Hulu, Southwest Yahukimo, East Yahukimo, North Yahukimo and Ghondumi Sisare, and the new cities will be the municipalities of Merauke and Lembah Baliem (Baliem Valley).</p>
<p>The two new provinces from parts of the existing Papua province have recently been approved by Indonesia's House of Representatives: South Papua and Central Papua. Another new province, to be split from <a href="/wiki/West_Papua_(province)" title="West Papua (province)">West Papua</a> will be Southwest Papua.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The proposed South Papua (<i>Papua Selatan</i>) Province would cover an area of 119,749 square km, which is rich in natural resources. It will encompass four existing regencies – Asmat, Boven Digoel, Mappi, and Merauke (including Merauke City, scheduled to become a municipality) and will thus equate closely to the <i>original</i> Merauke Regency prior to the splitting of that entity in 2002. Within the existing regencies, new regencies to be added are those of Moyo (from Boven Digoel Regency), Muara Digul and Admi Korbay (both from Mappi Regency).</p>
<p>According to a 20 January 2012 report in the <i>Cenderawasih Pos Jakarta</i>, the central government is moving forward with the creation of "Central Papua". At that time it was envisaged that the new province would comprise ten existing regencies: Supiori, Biak Numfor, Yapen Islands, Waropen, Nabire, Dogiyai, Deiyai, Intan Jaya, Paniai and Mimika.</p>
<p>The new Central Papua Province, and the residual Papua Province, would together include the new regencies of Gili Menawa (from Jayapura Regency), Balin Senter (from Lanny Jaya Regency and Tolikara Regency), Boboga (from Tolikara Regency), Puncak Trikora (from Lanny Jaya Regency), Katengban (from Bintang Mountains Regency), Okika (from Jayawijaya Regency), Northwest Yapen and East Yapen (both from Yapen Islands Regency), Numfor Island (from Biak Numfor Regency), Yalimek, Ser Yahukimo Western Mountains, Mambera Hulu, Southwest Yahukimo, East Yahukimo and North Yakuhimo (all six from Yahukimo Regency) and Ghondumi Sisare (from Waropen Regency), and a new municipality of Lembah Baliem (Baliem Valley) created from Jayawijaya Regency.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jayapura_City">Jayapura City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papua_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Jayapura City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city of <a href="/wiki/Jayapura" title="Jayapura">Jayapura</a> also has the status of an autonomous city, equal to a regency. It was founded on 7 March 1910 as <i>Hollandia</i> and is the capital. Since Indonesian administration the name of the city has been changed to Kotabaru, then to Sukarnopura before its current name, Jayapura.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Jayapura is also the largest city of Papua Province, with a small but active tourism industry.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2011)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is built on a slope overlooking the bay. <a href="/wiki/Cenderawasih_University" title="Cenderawasih University">Cenderawasih University</a> (<i>UNCEN</i>) campus at Abepura houses the <a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Museum_(Cenderawasih_University)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="University Museum (Cenderawasih University) (page does not exist)">University Museum</a> where some of the <a href="/wiki/Asmat_people" title="Asmat people">Asmat</a> artifacts collected by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Rockefeller" title="Michael Rockefeller">Michael Rockefeller</a> is stored.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Both <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tanjung_Ria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tanjung Ria (page does not exist)">Tanjung Ria</a> beach, near the market at Hamadi&#160;– site of 22 April 1944 <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> invasion during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>&#160;– and the site of General <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a>'s World War II headquarters at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ifar_Gunung&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ifar Gunung (page does not exist)">Ifar Gunung</a> have monuments commemorating the events.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papua_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A central east–west mountain range dominates the geography of the island of New Guinea, over 1,600&#160;km (1,000&#160;mi) in total length. The western section is around 600&#160;km (400&#160;mi) long and 100&#160;km (60&#160;mi) across.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The province contains the highest mountains between the <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalayas</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a>, rising up to 4,884 metres (16,024&#160;ft) high, and ensuring a steady supply of rain from the tropical atmosphere.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tree_line" title="Tree line">tree line</a> is around 4,000 metres (13,000&#160;ft) elevation and the tallest peaks contain permanent equatorial glaciers,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> increasingly melting due to a <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">changing climate</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Various other smaller mountain ranges occur both north and west of the central ranges.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Except in high elevations, most areas possess a hot humid climate throughout the year, with some seasonal variation associated with the <a href="/wiki/Monsoon#Northeast_monsoon" title="Monsoon">northeast monsoon</a> season.</p>
<p>The southern and northern lowlands stretch for hundreds of kilometres and include lowland rainforests, extensive <a href="/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> grasslands, and expanses of <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangrove</a> forest.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The southern lowlands are the site of <a href="/wiki/Lorentz_National_Park" title="Lorentz National Park">Lorentz National Park</a>, also a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO_World_Heritage_Site" class="mw-redirect" title="UNESCO World Heritage Site">UNESCO World Heritage Site</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The province's largest river is the <a href="/wiki/Mamberamo" class="mw-redirect" title="Mamberamo">Mamberamo</a> located in the northern part of the province.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The result is a large area of lakes and rivers known as the Lakes Plains region.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a href="/wiki/Baliem_Valley" title="Baliem Valley">Baliem Valley</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/Dani_(ethnic_group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dani (ethnic group)">Dani</a> people, is a tableland 1,600 metres (5,200&#160;ft) above sea level in the midst of the central mountain range.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Puncak_Jaya" title="Puncak Jaya">Puncak Jaya</a>, also known by its Dutch colonial name, "Carstensz Pyramid", is a <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> mountain peak 4,884 metres (16,024&#160;ft) above sea level.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is the highest peak of Oceania.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p><br />
The Yei (pronounced Yay) are sometimes known as the Jei, Je, Yei-nan people.</p>
<p>There are approximately 2,500 speakers of the Yei language. 40% Ethno Religionists- animistic tribal religion 60% Catholics and other Christians (blended with animistic beliefs &amp; customs): The Yei language is believed to have two dialects observed by a Wycliffe, SIL language survey in 2001. At home the Yei people speak their own language but use Indonesian for trade, wider communication and at school. Most Yei are literate in Indonesian.</p>
<p>There are elementary schools in each village. About 10–30% of children continue in middle school. Very few go to high school. The nearest high school is in <a href="/wiki/Merauke" title="Merauke">Merauke</a> city. They live primarily by hunting, fishing, and gardening short and long term crops in the lowlands. The Yei diet mainly consists of rice, vegetables, fish and roasted <a href="/wiki/Sago" title="Sago">sago</a>. With their land at an altitude of less than 100 meters above sea level, the Yei people can best be accessed by vehicle on the road from Merauke or by motorized canoe up the <a href="/wiki/Maro_River" title="Maro River">Maro River</a>. There is no airstrip or airplane access other than float plane which is currently available from Merauke through MAF by about a 15-minute flight to Toray. The Poo and Bupul villages have a clinic but people still use traditional medicines. There is very little infrastructure in the area: no telephones or toilets. At night electricity is run from a generator. There are single side-band radios (SSBs) in Bupul, Tanas, Poo, and Erambu villages, mainly used by the police and military force. Most villages get their drinking water from the Maro River, but some get it from wells or by collecting rain.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>The population of Papua province has a fertility rate of 2.9 children per woman<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The population grew from the 1.9&#160;million recorded in the <a href="/wiki/Indonesia_2000_census" title="Indonesia 2000 census">2000 Indonesia Census</a>, to 2.9&#160;million as recorded by the 2010 Census,<sup id="cite_ref-JKTPOS_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKTPOS-12">[12]</a></sup> and is officially estimated to be at about 3.5 million in 2014. Since the early 1990s Papua has had the highest population growth rate of all Indonesian provinces at over 3% annually.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This is partly a result of birth rates, but mainly due to migration from other parts of Indonesia.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> While indigenous Papuans formed the near-totality of the population in 1961, they are now roughly 50% of the population,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> the other half being composed of non-Papuan migrants coming from other parts of Indonesia. An overwhelming percentage of these migrants came as part of <a href="/wiki/Transmigrasi" class="mw-redirect" title="Transmigrasi">a government-sponsored transmigration program</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>According to the 2010 census, 83.15% of the Papuans identified themselves as Christian with 65.48% being Protestant and 17.67% being Roman Catholic. 15.89% of the population was Muslim and less than 1% were Buddhist or Hindu.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> There is also substantial practice of <a href="/wiki/Animism" title="Animism">animism</a> by Papuans.</p>
<p>The densest population center, other than the large coastal cities that house Indonesian bureaucratic and commercial apparatus, is located in and around the town of <a href="/wiki/Wamena" title="Wamena">Wamena</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Baliem_Valley" title="Baliem Valley">Baliem Valley</a> of the Central Highlands.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papua_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, Papuan caretaker governor Syamsul Arief Rivai claimed Papua's forests cover 42 million hectares with an estimated worth of Rp.700&#160;trillion ($78&#160;billion) and that if the forests were managed properly and sustainably, they could produce over 500&#160;million cubic meters of logs per annum.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Grasberg_Mine" class="mw-redirect" title="Grasberg Mine">Grasberg Mine</a>, the world's largest gold mine and third largest copper mine,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> is located in the highlands near <a href="/wiki/Puncak_Jaya" title="Puncak Jaya">Puncak Jaya</a>, the highest mountain in Papua.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papua_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Paradisaea_apoda_-Bali_Bird_Park-7.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Paradisaea_apoda_-Bali_Bird_Park-7.jpg/170px-Paradisaea_apoda_-Bali_Bird_Park-7.jpg" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Paradisaea_apoda_-Bali_Bird_Park-7.jpg/255px-Paradisaea_apoda_-Bali_Bird_Park-7.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Paradisaea_apoda_-Bali_Bird_Park-7.jpg/340px-Paradisaea_apoda_-Bali_Bird_Park-7.jpg 2x" data-file-width="683" data-file-height="1024" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Paradisaea_apoda_-Bali_Bird_Park-7.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i><a href="/wiki/Paradisaea_apoda" class="mw-redirect" title="Paradisaea apoda">Paradisaea apoda</a></i>, native to Papua, displaying its feathers</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The island has an estimated 16,000 species of plant, 124 genera of which are endemic.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Papua's known forest fauna includes; <a href="/wiki/Marsupials" class="mw-redirect" title="Marsupials">marsupials</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Phalangeriformes" title="Phalangeriformes">possums</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wallaby" title="Wallaby">wallabies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tree-kangaroo" title="Tree-kangaroo">tree-kangaroos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuscus" title="Cuscus">cuscuses</a>); other mammals (including the endangered <a href="/wiki/Long-beaked_echidna" title="Long-beaked echidna">long-beaked echidna</a>); bird species such as <a href="/wiki/Birds-of-paradise" class="mw-redirect" title="Birds-of-paradise">birds-of-paradise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cassowary" title="Cassowary">cassowaries</a>, parrots, and <a href="/wiki/Cockatoo" title="Cockatoo">cockatoos</a>; the world's longest lizards (Papua <a href="/wiki/Monitor_lizard" title="Monitor lizard">monitor</a>); and the world's largest butterflies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The waterways and wetlands of Papua are also home to salt and freshwater <a href="/wiki/Crocodile" title="Crocodile">crocodile</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tree_monitors&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tree monitors (page does not exist)">tree monitors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Megabat" title="Megabat">flying foxes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osprey" title="Osprey">osprey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bats</a> and other animals;<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> while the equatorial glacier fields remain largely unexplored.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Protected areas within Papua province include the World Heritage <a href="/wiki/Lorentz_National_Park" title="Lorentz National Park">Lorentz National Park</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Wasur_National_Park" title="Wasur National Park">Wasur National Park</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Ramsar_site" title="Ramsar site">Ramsar</a> wetland of international importance.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In February 2006, a team of scientists exploring the <a href="/wiki/Foja_Mountains" title="Foja Mountains">Foja Mountains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarmi" title="Sarmi">Sarmi</a>, discovered new species of birds, butterflies, amphibians, and plants, including possibly the largest-flowered species of <a href="/wiki/Rhododendron" title="Rhododendron">rhododendron</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ecological threats include logging-induced <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>, forest conversion for plantation agriculture (including <a href="/wiki/Palm_oil" title="Palm oil">oil palm</a>), smallholder agricultural conversion, the introduction and potential spread of alien species such as the <a href="/wiki/Crab-eating_macaque" title="Crab-eating macaque">crab-eating macaque</a> which preys on and competes with indigenous species, the illegal species trade, and water pollution from oil and mining operations.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
