<p><b>Hakkâri Province</b> (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>Hakkâri ili</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Kurdish_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Kurdish language">Kurdish</a>: <span lang="ku" dir="rtl" xml:lang="ku">Parêzgeha Hekarî</span>‎) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Turkey" title="Provinces of Turkey">province</a> in the south east corner of <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. The administrative centre is located in the city of <a href="/wiki/Hakk%C3%A2ri" title="Hakkâri">Hakkâri</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kurdish_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Kurdish language">Kurdish</a>: <span lang="ku" dir="rtl" xml:lang="ku">Colemêrg</span>‎). The province covers an area of 7,121&#160;km² and has a population of 251,302 (2010 est). The province had a population of 236,581 in 2000. The province was created in 1936 out of part of <a href="/wiki/Van_Province" title="Van Province">Van Province</a>. Its adjacent provinces are <a href="/wiki/%C5%9E%C4%B1rnak_Province" title="Şırnak Province">Şırnak</a> to the west and <a href="/wiki/Van_Province" title="Van Province">Van</a> to the north. The majority of the province's population is Kurdish.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>The area has been ruled by <a href="/wiki/Gutians" class="mw-redirect" title="Gutians">Gutians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurrians" title="Hurrians">Hurrians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Akkadians" class="mw-redirect" title="Akkadians">Akkadians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hittites" title="Hittites">Hittites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Babylon" title="Babylon">Babylon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macedonians_(Greeks)" title="Macedonians (Greeks)">Macedonians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persian_people" title="Persian people">Persians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greeks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenians" title="Armenians">Armenians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parthians" class="mw-redirect" title="Parthians">Parthians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Byzantium" title="Byzantium">Byzantium</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sassanids" class="mw-redirect" title="Sassanids">Sassanids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seljuks" class="mw-redirect" title="Seljuks">Seljuks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongols</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ottomans" class="mw-redirect" title="Ottomans">Ottomans</a> in its long history. Prior to the <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_genocide" title="Assyrian genocide">Assyrian genocide</a>, the Hakkari region was home to a mixed population of Assyrians and Kurds,<sup id="cite_ref-Becker_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Becker-3">[3]</a></sup> and the following list concerns the Assyrian tribes that inhabited Hakkari (note: this list is incomplete):</p>
