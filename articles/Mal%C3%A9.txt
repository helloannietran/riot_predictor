<p><b>Malé</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/./ syllable break">.</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span>, <small>local pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[ˈmɑːlɛ]</a></span> <a href="/wiki/Maldivian_language" title="Maldivian language">Maldivian</a>: <span lang="dv" xml:lang="dv">މާލެ</span>) is the <a href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> and most populous city in the <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Republic of Maldives</a>. With a population of 133,412<sup id="cite_ref-population-census2014_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-population-census2014-1">[1]</a></sup> and an area of 5.8<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>km<sup>2</sup>, it is also one of the <a href="/wiki/Most_densely_populated_cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Most densely populated cities">most densely populated cities in the world</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The city is geographically located at the southern edge of North Malé Atoll (<a href="/wiki/Kaafu_Atoll" title="Kaafu Atoll">Kaafu Atoll</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Administratively, the city consists of a central island, an airport island, and two other islands governed by the Malé City Council.</p>
<p>Traditionally it was the King's Island, from where the ancient <a href="/wiki/List_of_sultans_of_the_Maldives" title="List of sultans of the Maldives">royal dynasties</a> ruled and where the palace was located. The city was then called <i>Mahal</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Formerly it was a walled city surrounded by fortifications and gates (<i>doroshi</i>). The Royal Palace (<i>Gan'duvaru</i>) was destroyed along with the picturesque forts (<i>kotte</i>) and bastions (<i>buruzu</i>) when the city was remodelled under President <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Nasir" title="Ibrahim Nasir">Ibrahim Nasir</a>'s rule in the aftermath of the abolition of the monarchy in 1968. However, the <a href="/wiki/Mal%C3%A9_Friday_Mosque" title="Malé Friday Mosque">Malé Friday Mosque</a> remains. In recent years, the island has been considerably expanded through land-filling operations. Over the years, Malé has been the center of political protests and milestone events.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mal%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although Malé is geographically located in <a href="/wiki/Kaafu_Atoll" title="Kaafu Atoll">Kaafu Atoll</a>, administratively it is not considered part of it. The central part of the city is formed by the island of Malé. Three more islands form part of the city. A commercial harbour is located on the central island and serves as the heart of all commercial activities in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Pub-173_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pub-173-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The central island is heavily <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urbanized</a>, with the built-up area taking up essentially its entire landmass.<sup id="cite_ref-Pub-173_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pub-173-5">[5]</a></sup> Slightly less than one third of the nation's population lives in the capital city, and the population has increased from 20,000 people in 1987 to 100,000 people in 2006. Many Maldivians and foreign workers living in other parts of the country find themselves in occasional short term residence on the island since it is the centre of administration and bureaucracy.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mal%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The whole island group, the Maldives, is named after its capital. The word "Maldives" means "the islands (dives) of Malé'".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mal%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first settlers in the Maldivian islands were <a href="/wiki/Dravidian_people" title="Dravidian people">Dravidian people</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> who arrived from the neighboring shores of the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Tamil_country" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient Tamil country">modern Indian Subcontinent and coastal Ceylon</a>. Comparative studies of Maldivian linguistic, oral, and other cultural traditions, in addition to <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">folklore</a>, point to a strong Dravidian influence on Maldivian society, centered in Malé, from ancient times. The people of <a href="/wiki/Giraavaru_(Kaafu_Atoll)" title="Giraavaru (Kaafu Atoll)">Giraavaru</a>, an island located in Malé Atoll (now a tourist resort, after the forced diaspora of its inhabitants) claim to descend from the first settlers of the Maldives, none other than the ancient <a href="/wiki/Tamil_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Tamil people">Tamils</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is said that Giraavaru fishermen used to go regularly to a certain large sandbank (finolhu) at the southern end of their atoll to clean tuna fish after a good catch. Owing to the large amount of tuna fish offal and blood, the waters around that sandbank looked like a big pool of blood (maa ley gandeh). "Maa" (from the <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> मह "Maha"), meaning big, and "Lē" meaning blood. Traditionally the first inhabitants of the Maldives, which include the Giravaru people, didn't have kings. They lived in a simple society and were ruled by local headmen.</p>
<p>However, one day, a prince from the Subcontinent called Koimala arrived in the Malé Atoll sailing from the North on a big ship. The people of Giraavaru spotted his vessel from afar and welcomed him. They allowed Prince Koimala to settle on that large sandbank in the midst of the waters tainted with fish blood. Trees were planted on the sandbank and it is said that the first tree that grew on it was the <a href="/wiki/Papaya" title="Papaya">papaya</a> tree. However, this could refer to any tree that bears edible fruit as the archaic Dhivehi word (and <a href="/wiki/Mahal_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Mahal language">Mahal</a> word even today) for fruit was the same as that for the papaya (<i>falhoa</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> As time went by the local islanders accepted the rule of this Northern Prince. A palace was built and the island was formally named Maa-le (Malé), while the nearest island was named Hulhu-le.</p>
<p>The names of the main four wards or divisions of Malé Island are said to have been given by the aboriginal Giraavaru fishermen: Maafannu from "maa" (big) and "fannu" (a place where a village path meets the sea), Henveiru from "en-beyru" (out where fishermen got their bait), Galolhu from "galu-olhu" (stone groove) and, Macchangolhi from "mathi-angolhi" (windward path-fork).</p>
<p>The modern-day city was founded as a trading post by the Portuguese in the 16th century.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mal%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mal%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Malé has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_monsoon_climate" title="Tropical monsoon climate">tropical monsoon climate</a> under the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>. The city features a mix of both <a href="/wiki/Wet_season" title="Wet season">wet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dry_season" title="Dry season">dry seasons</a>, with the wet season lasting from May through December and the dry season covering the remaining four months. Unlike a number of cities with this climate, Malé experiences relatively consistent temperatures throughout the course of the year, with an average high of 30 degrees Celsius and an average low of 26.5 degrees Celsius, which is equivalent to many equatorial cities' average year round daily mean. The city averages slightly more than 1600&#160;mm of precipitation annually.</p>

<p>The city is divided into six divisions, four of which are on Malé Island: Henveiru, Galolhu, Maafannu and Macchangolhi. The nearby island of Vilingili, formerly a tourist resort and prior to that a prison, is the fifth division (Vilimalé). The sixth division is <a href="/wiki/Hulhumal%C3%A9" title="Hulhumalé">Hulhumalé</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Artificial_island" title="Artificial island">artificial island</a> settled since 2004. In addition, the <a href="/wiki/Mal%C3%A9_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Malé International Airport">airport Island</a> Hulhule is part of the city. Plans have been made to develop the <i>Gulhi Falu</i> reef, implementation began in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Island of Malé is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_islands_by_population_density" title="List of islands by population density">fifth most densely populated island in the world</a>, and it is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_islands_by_population" title="List of islands by population">168th most populous island</a> in the world. Since there is no surrounding countryside, all infrastructure has to be located in the city itself. Water is provided from desalinated ground water; the water works pumps brackish water from 50-60m deep wells in the city and desalinates that using <a href="/wiki/Reverse_osmosis" title="Reverse osmosis">reverse osmosis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WS-FAQ_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WS-FAQ-16">[16]</a></sup> Electric power is generated in the city using <a href="/wiki/Diesel_generator" title="Diesel generator">diesel</a> generators.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Sewage is pumped unprocessed into the sea.<sup id="cite_ref-WS-FAQ_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WS-FAQ-16">[16]</a></sup> Solid waste is transported to nearby islands, where it is used to fill in lagoons. The <a href="/wiki/Mal%C3%A9_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Malé International Airport">airport</a> was built in this way, and currently the <a href="/wiki/Thilafushi" title="Thilafushi">Thilafushi</a> lagoon is being filled in.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Many government buildings and agencies are located on the waterfront. <a href="/wiki/Mal%C3%A9_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Malé International Airport">Malé International Airport</a> is on adjacent <a href="/wiki/Hulhule_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Hulhule Island">Hulhule Island</a> which includes a seaplane base for internal transportation. Several land reclamation projects have expanded the harbour.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mal%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Tourism is the largest industry in the Maldives, accounting for 28% of GDP and more than 60% of the Maldives' foreign exchange receipts. The GDP per capita expanded by 265% in the 1980s and a further 115% in the 1990s. Over 90% of government tax revenue comes from import duties and tourism-related taxes. Malé, the capital, has many tourist attractions and nearby resorts. The central harbour and port of the Maldives is located in Malé, the centre for all commercial activities. <a href="/wiki/Maldivian_(airline)" title="Maldivian (airline)">Maldivian</a>, the airline of the Maldives, has its head office in Malé<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> as does the airline <a href="/wiki/FlyMe_(Maldivian_airline)" class="mw-redirect" title="FlyMe (Maldivian airline)">FlyMe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Nasir_International_Airport" title="Ibrahim Nasir International Airport">Ibrahim Nasir International Airport</a>, on nearby <a href="/wiki/Hulhul%C3%A9_Island" title="Hulhulé Island">Hulhulé Island</a>, is the city's airport and the principal airport in Maldives.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="City_Council">City Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mal%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: City Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Malé City Council is the <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">local government</a> body responsible for the governance of the city of Malé. The council was created in 2011, with the enactment of the Decentralization Bill, which saw the introduction of local governance to the country.</p>
<p>The city is divided into 11 political <a href="/wiki/Ward_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ward (politics)">wards</a> each with one <a href="/wiki/Councillor" title="Councillor">councillor</a>. The majority of current councillors, elected in the country's second local council elections in 2014, is from the <a href="/wiki/Maldivian_Democratic_Party" title="Maldivian Democratic Party">Maldivian Democratic Party</a>.</p>
