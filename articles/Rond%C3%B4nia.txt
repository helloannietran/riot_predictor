<p><b>Rondônia</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[ʁõˈdõniɐ]</a></span><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/States_of_Brazil" title="States of Brazil">state</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, located in the north-western part of the country. To the west is a short border with the state of <a href="/wiki/Acre_(state)" title="Acre (state)">Acre</a>, to the north is the state of <a href="/wiki/Amazonas,_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazonas, Brazil">Amazonas</a>, in the east is <a href="/wiki/Mato_Grosso" title="Mato Grosso">Mato Grosso</a>, and in the south and southwest is <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Porto_Velho" title="Porto Velho">Porto Velho</a>. The state was named after <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A2ndido_Rondon" title="Cândido Rondon">Cândido Rondon</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rond%C3%B4nia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rondonia was originally home to over 200,000 km<sup>2</sup> of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rainforest" title="Tropical rainforest">rainforest</a>, but has become one of the most <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforested</a> places in the Amazon. By 2003 around 70,000 km<sup>2</sup> of rainforest had been cleared.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The area around the <a href="/wiki/Guapor%C3%A9_River" title="Guaporé River">Guaporé River</a> is part of the <a href="/wiki/Beni_savanna" class="mw-redirect" title="Beni savanna">Beni savanna</a> ecoregion.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Hydroelectric_Dam" title="Samuel Hydroelectric Dam">Samuel Dam</a> is located in the state, on the <a href="/wiki/Jamari_River" title="Jamari River">Jamari River</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rond%C3%B4nia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Institute_of_Geography_and_Statistics" title="Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics">IBGE</a> of 2008, there were 1,519,000 people residing in the state. The population density was 6.6 inh./km². <a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">Urbanization</a>: 66.8% (2004); <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">Population growth</a>: 2.2% (1991-2000); <a href="/wiki/House" title="House">Houses</a>: 430,747 (2005).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The last PNAD (National Research for Sample of Domiciles) census revealed the following numbers: 832,000 <a href="/wiki/Brown_people#Pardos_in_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="Brown people">Brown</a> (<a href="/wiki/Multiracial#Brazil" title="Multiracial">Multiracial</a>) people (54.81%), 546,000 <a href="/wiki/White_Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="White Brazilian">White</a> people (35.95%), 115,000 <a href="/wiki/Afro-Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="Afro-Brazilian">Black</a> people (7.56%), 16,000 <a href="/wiki/Asian_Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian Brazilian">Asian</a> people (1.08%), 8,000 <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_in_Brazil" title="Indigenous peoples in Brazil">Amerindian</a> people (0.53%).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
