<p><b>San Antonio de Padua de la Concordia</b> (usually shortened to <b>Concordia</b>) is a city in the north-east of the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Argentina" title="Provinces of Argentina">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Entre_R%C3%ADos_Province" title="Entre Ríos Province">Entre Ríos</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia,_Argentina" title="Mesopotamia, Argentina">Mesopotamia</a>. It had about 172,000 inhabitants at the 2001 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> <small>[<a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Statistics_and_Census_of_Argentina" title="National Institute of Statistics and Census of Argentina">INDEC</a>]</small>, and is the head town of the <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Argentina" title="Departments of Argentina">department</a> of the same name.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Concordia,_Entre_R%C3%ADos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Concordia lies on the right-hand (western) shore of the <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_River" title="Uruguay River">Uruguay River</a>, opposite the city of <a href="/wiki/Salto,_Uruguay" title="Salto, Uruguay">Salto</a> in <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>. The two cities are joined by <a href="/wiki/Salto_Grande_Bridge" title="Salto Grande Bridge">a road/railway link</a> that is part of the <a href="/wiki/Salto_Grande_Dam" title="Salto Grande Dam">Salto Grande Dam</a> complex (starting on the Argentine side 18&#160;km north from the center of Concordia).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nature">Nature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Concordia,_Entre_R%C3%ADos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Nature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city is known as the national capital of <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a> production. <a href="/wiki/El_Palmar_National_Park" title="El Palmar National Park">El Palmar National Park</a>, an important reserve for Yatay <a href="/wiki/Palm_tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Palm tree">palm trees</a>, lies 60&#160;km south of the city.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Concordia,_Entre_R%C3%ADos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area is served by an <a href="/wiki/Comodoro_Pierrestegui_Airport" title="Comodoro Pierrestegui Airport">airport</a> (<a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code" title="International Air Transport Association airport code">IATA</a>: <b>COC</b>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization_airport_code" title="International Civil Aviation Organization airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>SAAC</b>) located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Concordia%2C_Entre_R%C3%ADos&amp;params=31_18_S_58_0_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">31°18′S</span> <span class="longitude">58°0′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">31.300°S 58.000°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">-31.300; -58.000</span></span></span></a></span>, but which is ostensibly not operational.</p>
