<p><b>Nørrebro</b> is one of the 10 official districts of <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-K.C3.B8benhavns_bydele_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-K.C3.B8benhavns_bydele-1">[1]</a></sup> It is northwest of the <a href="/wiki/Indre_By" title="Indre By">city centre</a>, beyond the location of the old Northern Gate (<i>Nørreport</i>), which, until dismantled in 1856, was near the current <a href="/wiki/N%C3%B8rreport_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Nørreport station">Nørreport station</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%B8rrebro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nørrebro has an area of 3.82&#160;km² and a population of 71,891.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Indre_By" title="Indre By">Indre By</a> to the southeast, <a href="/wiki/%C3%98sterbro" title="Østerbro">Østerbro</a> to the northeast, <a href="/wiki/Bispebjerg" title="Bispebjerg">Bispebjerg</a> to the northwest and <a href="/wiki/Frederiksberg_Municipality" title="Frederiksberg Municipality">Frederiksberg Municipality</a> to the southwest.<sup id="cite_ref-K.C3.B8benhavns_bydele_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-K.C3.B8benhavns_bydele-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%B8rrebro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before 1852, Nørrebro was in the countryside. When the city decided to abandon the demarcation line in 1852, which had previously kept the city within very limited geographical limits, a building boom took place in Nørrebro. Nørrebro became the home of thousands of new workers, who came to seek their fortune in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%B8rrebro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2015, 26% of the residents in Nørrebro were immigrants or descendents of immigrants, 15% from outside Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nørrebro is known for its poly-cultural society, mostly of Middle Eastern origin. The dynamic, <a href="/wiki/Multiethnic_society" class="mw-redirect" title="Multiethnic society">multiethnic</a> main street <i>Nørrebrogade</i> runs through the area, with a multitude of shops and restaurants. One of the main points of interest in the area is historic <a href="/wiki/Assistants_Cemetery_(Copenhagen)" class="mw-redirect" title="Assistants Cemetery (Copenhagen)">Assistens Cemetery</a> (<i>Assistens Kirkegård</i>), the final resting place of famous Danes such as <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8ren_Kierkegaard" title="Søren Kierkegaard">Søren Kierkegaard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niels_Bohr" title="Niels Bohr">Niels Bohr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hans_Christian_Andersen" title="Hans Christian Andersen">H.C. Andersen</a>.</p>
<p>Nørrebro is inhabited by people from all parts of the world. The largest minority groups of people living in Nørrebro are <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turks_(ethnic_group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Turks (ethnic group)">Turks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistanis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bosnia" class="mw-redirect" title="Bosnia">Bosnians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albanians</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Riots">Riots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%B8rrebro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Riots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gadewar.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Gadewar.jpg/220px-Gadewar.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Gadewar.jpg/330px-Gadewar.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/7d/Gadewar.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="266" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gadewar.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Protesters fighting police in Nørrebro</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>Nørrebrogade</i> is known as the site of many <a href="/wiki/Riot" title="Riot">riots</a> over the years. During the 1980s, it often provided the setting for violent clashes between <a href="/wiki/Danish_police" class="mw-redirect" title="Danish police">Danish police</a> and militant <a href="/wiki/Squatting" title="Squatting">squatters</a> known as BZ. The battles were of a very vicious nature, often involving <a href="/wiki/Molotov-cocktail" class="mw-redirect" title="Molotov-cocktail">Molotov-cocktails</a> and pipe bombs being used by the squatters, as well as batons, tear gas and firearms used by the police.</p>
<p>On 18 May 1993, the district was the scene of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%B8rrebro_riot&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nørrebro riot (page does not exist)">Nørrebro riot</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Maastricht_Treaty_referendum,_1993" title="Danish Maastricht Treaty referendum, 1993">Danish "yes"-vote to the European Union</a>. The police were unprepared for the rioters, who threw paving stones from a nearby construction site. Subsequently, 113 shots were fired: several demonstrators were wounded, some severely.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Though none of the protesters died at the time, the fight left 13 demonstrators and 92 policemen injured.</p>
<p>The riot was the worst since <a href="/wiki/Occupation_of_Denmark" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupation of Denmark">World War II</a>, but although an official investigation was initiated<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> there is still much doubt as to why the policemen were unprepared or what exactly happened.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Following the riot the Danish police upgraded their hand-carried weapons and <a href="/wiki/Riot_gear" class="mw-redirect" title="Riot gear">riot gear</a> to modern standards and began a process towards better strategic and tactical handling of crowds and riots.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_December_Riot" title="Copenhagen December Riot">late December 2006</a>, a riot took place, regarding the fate of the squatted social centre <a href="/wiki/Ungdomshuset" title="Ungdomshuset">Ungdomshuset</a> ("The Youth House"), a rendez-vous point for the far-left. A demonstration in support of the house was blocked by the police and a fullface streetfight broke out. The demonstrators hurled stones and fireworks at the police, who in response used their armoured cars to disband the demonstrators and fired <a href="/wiki/Tear_gas" title="Tear gas">tear gas</a> in to the crowd. The fight grew into a full-scale riot, with fires burning over night in the streets. 4 police and 2 demonstrators were injured.</p>
<p>On March 1, 2007, the <a href="/wiki/March_2007_Denmark_Riots" class="mw-redirect" title="March 2007 Denmark Riots">biggest riots in recent Danish history</a> broke out when the police moved in and evicted squatters in Ungdomshuset, which was followed by the demolition of the building. It is estimated that the eviction of Ungdomshuset and the damage done during the following riots has cost the city and the state around 100 million <a href="/wiki/Danish_kroner" class="mw-redirect" title="Danish kroner">danish kroner</a> (around 17 million <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>). On September 1, 2007, additional rioting resulted from a protest marking six months after the demolition of Ungdomshuset.<sup id="cite_ref-protest070901_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-protest070901-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
