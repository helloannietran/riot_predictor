<p><b>Montreal North</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i><b>Montréal-Nord</b></i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">borough</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Arrondissement" title="Arrondissement">arrondissement</a></i>) of the city of <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It consists entirely of the former city of Montréal-Nord on <a href="/wiki/Island_of_Montreal" title="Island of Montreal">Island of Montreal</a> in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. It was amalgamated into the City of <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> on January 1, 2002.</p>
<p>Montreal North has the reputation of being one of Montreal's most dangerous boroughs, along with <a href="/wiki/Hochelaga-Maisonneuve" title="Hochelaga-Maisonneuve">Hochelaga-Maisonneuve</a>. The area contains a sizable community living below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a> line, though it also has a middle-class and upper-middle-class residents.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montr%C3%A9al-Nord&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Parc_Montreal-Nord.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Parc_Montreal-Nord.jpg/220px-Parc_Montreal-Nord.jpg" width="220" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Parc_Montreal-Nord.jpg/330px-Parc_Montreal-Nord.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Parc_Montreal-Nord.jpg/440px-Parc_Montreal-Nord.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1584" data-file-height="1200" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Parc_Montreal-Nord.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Pierre_Granche" title="Pierre Granche">Pierre Granche</a>'s sculpture in park in Montréal-Nord.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The borough is an oblong municipal division situated along the <a href="/wiki/Rivi%C3%A8re_des_Prairies" title="Rivière des Prairies">Rivière des Prairies</a>, in the northeastern part of the island.</p>
<p>It is bordered to the west by <a href="/wiki/Ahuntsic-Cartierville" title="Ahuntsic-Cartierville">Ahuntsic-Cartierville</a>, to the southwest by <a href="/wiki/Villeray%E2%80%93Saint-Michel%E2%80%93Parc-Extension" title="Villeray–Saint-Michel–Parc-Extension">Villeray–Saint-Michel–Parc-Extension</a>, to the south by <a href="/wiki/Saint_Leonard_(Montreal)" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Leonard (Montreal)">Saint Leonard</a>, at the southeast corner by <a href="/wiki/Anjou_(borough)" class="mw-redirect" title="Anjou (borough)">Anjou</a>, and to the east by <a href="/wiki/Rivi%C3%A8re-des-Prairies%E2%80%93Pointe-aux-Trembles" title="Rivière-des-Prairies–Pointe-aux-Trembles">Rivière-des-Prairies–Pointe-aux-Trembles</a>.</p>
<p>Major thoroughfares in Montreal North include Saint Michel Blvd., Pie IX Blvd. (<a href="/wiki/Quebec_Autoroute_25" title="Quebec Autoroute 25">Autoroute 25</a>), Lacordaire Blvd., Langelier Blvd., Léger Blvd., and Henri Bourassa Blvd. The <a href="/wiki/Pie_IX_Bridge" title="Pie IX Bridge">Pie IX Bridge</a> connects Montreal North to the <a href="/wiki/Laval,_Quebec" title="Laval, Quebec">Laval</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Saint-Vincent-de-Paul,_Quebec" title="Saint-Vincent-de-Paul, Quebec">Saint-Vincent-de-Paul</a>.</p>
<p>It has an area of 11.07&#160;km² and a population of 83,911.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montr%C3%A9al-Nord&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_and_provincial_elections">Federal and provincial elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montr%C3%A9al-Nord&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Federal and provincial elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The borough is located almost entirely in the federal riding of <a href="/wiki/Bourassa_(electoral_district)" title="Bourassa (electoral district)">Bourassa</a>, except for a tiny southeastern corner in <a href="/wiki/Honor%C3%A9-Mercier_(electoral_district)" title="Honoré-Mercier (electoral district)">Honoré-Mercier</a>.</p>
<p>The provincial electoral district of <a href="/wiki/Bourassa-Sauv%C3%A9_(Quebec_provincial_electoral_district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bourassa-Sauvé (Quebec provincial electoral district)">Bourassa-Sauvé</a> is coterminous with the borough except for a northwestern section in the electoral district of <a href="/wiki/Cr%C3%A9mazie_(electoral_district)" title="Crémazie (electoral district)">Crémazie</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Riot">Riot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montr%C3%A9al-Nord&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Riot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For yet undisclosed reasons on August 9, 2008 officers of the <a href="/wiki/Service_de_police_de_la_Ville_de_Montr%C3%A9al" title="Service de police de la Ville de Montréal">Service de police de la Ville de Montréal</a> shot and killed Honduran immigrant <a href="/wiki/Fredy_Alberto_Villanueva" class="mw-redirect" title="Fredy Alberto Villanueva">Fredy Alberto Villanueva</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cbc.ca_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbc.ca-9">[9]</a></sup> This served to set off the tensions that had already been simmering in this multi-ethnic borough, and riots erupted the next evening, after an otherwise peaceful protest march in the afternoon. Rioters looted neighborhood stores, several cars and garbage cans were set on fire, one paramedic and two police officers were wounded and one female police officer shot.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Firetrucks and firefighters arriving to put out the fires were then targeted by the rioters.<sup id="cite_ref-cbc.ca_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbc.ca-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montr%C3%A9al-Nord&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Primary_and_secondary_schools">Primary and secondary schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montr%C3%A9al-Nord&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Primary and secondary schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Commission_scolaire_de_la_Pointe-de-l%27%C3%8Ele" title="Commission scolaire de la Pointe-de-l'Île">Commission scolaire de la Pointe-de-l'Île</a> (CSPÎ) operates French-language public schools. The secondary schools in the arrondissement are <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_secondaire_Calixa-Lavall%C3%A9e" title="École secondaire Calixa-Lavallée">École secondaire Calixa-Lavallée</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Secondaire_Henri-Bourassa" title="École Secondaire Henri-Bourassa">École Secondaire Henri-Bourassa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> There is also the <i>Le Prélude</i> program.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> There are 14 Francophone primary schools in Montréal-Nord.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/English_Montreal_School_Board" title="English Montreal School Board">English Montreal School Board</a> operates Anglophone public schools:</p>
<ul>
<li>Galileo Adult Education Centre (<i>Centre d'éducation des adultes Galileo</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lester_B._Pearson_High_School_(Montreal)" title="Lester B. Pearson High School (Montreal)">Lester B Pearson High School</a></li>
<li>Gerald McShane School<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Prior to 1998 it was in the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Catholic_School_Commission" title="Montreal Catholic School Commission">Montreal Catholic School Commission</a> (CECM) and the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_School_Board_of_Greater_Montreal" title="Protestant School Board of Greater Montreal">Protestant School Board of Greater Montreal</a>, as at that time public schools were by religious denomination instead of on linguistic lines. In 1998 some schools in the eastern portion of CECM were transferred to the former area of <a href="/wiki/Commission_scolaire_J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me-Le_Royer" title="Commission scolaire Jérôme-Le Royer">Commission scolaire Jérôme-Le Royer</a>, which at that time became the territory of the CSPÎ.<sup id="cite_ref-Lagac.C3.A9Over40years_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lagac.C3.A9Over40years-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_libraries">Public libraries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montr%C3%A9al-Nord&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Public libraries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The borough has four libraries of the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Public_Libraries_Network" title="Montreal Public Libraries Network">Montreal Public Libraries Network</a>: Belleville, Bibliothèque de la Maison culturelle et communautaire, Charleroi, and Henri-Bourassa.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
