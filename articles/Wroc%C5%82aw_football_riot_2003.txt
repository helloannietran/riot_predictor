			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">2003 Wrocław football riot</h1>

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Wroc%C5%82aw" title="Wrocław">Wrocław</a> football riot 2003</b> was an organised fight between <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a> <a href="/wiki/Football_hooliganism" title="Football hooliganism">football hooligans</a> in Wrocław, <a href="/wiki/Lower_Silesia" title="Lower Silesia">Lower Silesia</a> Poland on 30 March 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2901451_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2901451-1">[1]</a></sup> As a result of the incident, one person died, 229 hooligans were arrested<sup id="cite_ref-WV1941_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV1941-2">[2]</a></sup> and more than twelve were taken to the hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-WV2107_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV2107-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Wroc%C5%82aw_football_riot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arranged football hooligan fights in Poland are known as <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/ustawka" class="extiw" title="wikt:ustawka">ustawka</a></i>. They became common in Poland in the late 1990s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Each club has a <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_hooligan_firms" title="List of hooligan firms">hooligan firm</a></i> and from these, different firms form alliances, supporting each other in their fights with other firms. There are two main alliances:</p>
<ul>
<li>The firms of <a href="/wiki/Arka_Gdynia" title="Arka Gdynia">Arka Gdynia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cracovia_(football_club)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cracovia (football club)">Cracovia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lech_Pozna%C5%84" title="Lech Poznań">Lech Poznań</a> - "Triad" (<a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <span lang="pl" xml:lang="pl"><i>Triada</i></span>)</li>
<li>The firms of <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Al%C4%85sk_Wroc%C5%82aw" title="Śląsk Wrocław">Śląsk Wrocław</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wis%C5%82a_Krak%C3%B3w" title="Wisła Kraków">Wisła Kraków</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lechia_Gda%C5%84sk" title="Lechia Gdańsk">Lechia Gdańsk</a> - <i>Three Kings of Great Cities</i> (<a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <span lang="pl" xml:lang="pl"><i>Trzej Królowie Wielkich Miast</i></span>)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_fight">The fight</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Wroc%C5%82aw_football_riot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The fight">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 30 March 2003 a <a href="/wiki/Poland_II_Liga" class="mw-redirect" title="Poland II Liga">Polish Second Division</a> game between Śląsk Wrocław and Arka Gdynia took place in Wrocław (<a href="/wiki/Stadion_Oporowska" title="Stadion Oporowska">Stadion Oporowska</a>). Hooligans from Wrocław, <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84" title="Poznań">Poznań</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krak%C3%B3w" title="Kraków">Kraków</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gdynia" title="Gdynia">Gdynia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lubin" title="Lubin">Lubin</a> all took part in the huge fight which was centred on Grabiszynska Street, 500 m from the stadium, using knives, cleavers, wooden clubs and stones.<sup id="cite_ref-WV1941_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV1941-2">[2]</a></sup> Triad was defeated and fled from the place of combat.</p>
<p>Wrocław <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a> later stated that the fight had been pre-arranged, adding that "They did not come to watch the game; they had made an appointment on their mobile phones to fight".<sup id="cite_ref-WV1941_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV1941-2">[2]</a></sup> Local residents had to hide in houses, shops and buses as the fight, which lasted some hours spread through the city. When the police arrived some of the hooligans attacked them with stones, before the police restored order,<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2901451_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2901451-1">[1]</a></sup> using water cannons on the rioters.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> After the arrival of a special police squad, the police finally surrounded most of the hooligans, ordering them to lie down, and 120 people were arrested.<sup id="cite_ref-WV1941_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV1941-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WV2107_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV2107-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ambulances took the injured to hospitals in Wrocław. A 24-year-old Arka Gdynia fan, named only as Mariusz B, who had been stabbed in the back, died in the hospital. Some witnesses claimed that he had fallen onto a street lamp, collapsed and was then beaten up.<sup id="cite_ref-WV1941_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV1941-2">[2]</a></sup> Of those taken to the hospital most had stab wounds, as well as fractures and contusions. However, most refused hospital treatment as they believed they would be arrested by the police. Rydygier hospital confirmed that some had fled the hospital and that one had a broken leg, another's shoulder was dislocated. Still another had stab wounds in his thigh, but all three refused to be hospitalized and left.<sup id="cite_ref-WV1941_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV1941-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WV2107_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV2107-3">[3]</a></sup> Others were seen at Babiński hospital with most of them also refusing treatment.<sup id="cite_ref-WV1941_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV1941-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a statement to the Polish State <a href="/wiki/News_agency" title="News agency">News agency</a>, <i>PAP</i>, The head of the <a href="/wiki/Polish_Football_Association" title="Polish Football Association">Polish Football Association</a>, Michal Listkiewicz, blamed the violence on what he called "bandits posing as fans", adding that, "They are murdering Polish soccer and all of our sport."<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2901451_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2901451-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Wroc%C5%82aw_football_riot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wrocław Police Department announced that 229 hooligans were arrested. 100 were subsequently banned from football matches in Poland for three years. 120 were indicted on charges of <a href="/wiki/Assault" title="Assault">assault</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Deadly_weapon" title="Deadly weapon">dangerous weapon</a> by the district prosecutor and 100 were found guilty.<sup id="cite_ref-WV2107_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WV2107-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The police had <a href="/wiki/Digital_evidence" title="Digital evidence">videotape evidence</a> from the fight, which has been handed to them by a local man. And so they knew the circumstances of the killing of the Arka Gdynia fan who had died of knife wounds, which had been inflicted by a man, named only as Tomasz P, a Wisła Kraków fan, who a few months later was caught hiding under a <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">false name</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rzesz%C3%B3w" title="Rzeszów">Rzeszów</a> in southeastern Poland. In October 2005 he was sentenced to ten years <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">imprisonment</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
