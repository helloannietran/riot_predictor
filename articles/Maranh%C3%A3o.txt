<p><b>Maranhão</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[mɐɾɐˈɲɐ̃w]</a></span>) is a northeastern <a href="/wiki/States_of_Brazil" title="States of Brazil">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. To the north lies the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. Maranhão is neighboured by the (clockwise from east) states of <a href="/wiki/Piau%C3%AD" title="Piauí">Piauí</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tocantins_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Tocantins State">Tocantins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Par%C3%A1" title="Pará">Pará</a>. The people of Maranhão have a distinctive accent inside the common Northeastern Brazilian dialect. Maranhão is described in books such as <i><a href="/wiki/Can%C3%A7%C3%A3o_do_ex%C3%ADlio" title="Canção do exílio">The Land of the Palm Trees</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Gon%C3%A7alves_Dias" title="Gonçalves Dias">Gonçalves Dias</a> and <i>Casa de Pensão</i> by <a href="/wiki/Alu%C3%ADsio_Azevedo" title="Aluísio Azevedo">Aluísio Azevedo</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Dune" title="Dune">dunes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Len%C3%A7%C3%B3is_Maranhenses" class="mw-redirect" title="Lençóis Maranhenses">Lençóis</a> are an important area of environmental preservation. Also of interest is the state capital of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o" title="São Luís, Maranhão">São Luís</a>, designated a Unesco World Heritage Site. Another important conservation area is the <a href="/wiki/Parna%C3%ADba_River" title="Parnaíba River">Parnaíba River</a> delta, between the states of Maranhão and <a href="/wiki/Piau%C3%AD" title="Piauí">Piauí</a>, with its <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> <a href="/wiki/Dune" title="Dune">dunes</a> and deserted <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beaches</a> or islands, such as the Caju <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a>, which shelters rare <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lencois_Maranhenses_3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Lencois_Maranhenses_3.jpg/220px-Lencois_Maranhenses_3.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Lencois_Maranhenses_3.jpg/330px-Lencois_Maranhenses_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Lencois_Maranhenses_3.jpg/440px-Lencois_Maranhenses_3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1944" /></a>

<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lencois_Maranhenses_3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lençóis Maranhenses.</div>

</div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Riopregui%C3%A7a.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Riopregui%C3%A7a.jpg/220px-Riopregui%C3%A7a.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Riopregui%C3%A7a.jpg/330px-Riopregui%C3%A7a.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Riopregui%C3%A7a.jpg/440px-Riopregui%C3%A7a.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="667" /></a>

Preguiça <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">River</a>.</div>

<p>The northern portion of the state is a heavily forested plain traversed by numerous rivers, occupied by the eastern extension of the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_moist_broadleaf_forests" title="Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests">tropical moist forests</a> of <a href="/wiki/Amazonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazonia">Amazonia</a>. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tocantins-Araguaia-Maranh%C3%A3o_moist_forests&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tocantins-Araguaia-Maranhão moist forests (page does not exist)">Tocantins-Araguaia-Maranhão moist forests</a> occupy the northwestern portion of the state, extending from the <a href="/wiki/Pindar%C3%A9_River" title="Pindaré River">Pindaré River</a> west into neighboring Pará state. The north-central and northeastern portion of the state, extending eastward into northern Piauí, is home to the <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o_Baba%C3%A7u_forests" title="Maranhão Babaçu forests">Maranhão Babaçu forests</a>, a degraded tropical moist forest ecoregion dominated by the <a href="/wiki/Baba%C3%A7u" class="mw-redirect" title="Babaçu">Babaçu</a> palm. Much of the forest has been cleared for cattle grazing and agriculture, and the Babaçu palm produces edible oil which is extracted commercially.</p>
<p>The southern portion of the state belong to the lower terraces of the great <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Highlands" title="Brazilian Highlands">Brazilian Highlands</a>, occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Cerrado" title="Cerrado">Cerrado</a> savannas. Several plateau <a href="/wiki/Escarpment" title="Escarpment">escarpments</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Chapada_das_Mangabeiras" title="Chapada das Mangabeiras">Chapada das Mangabeiras</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serra_do_Tiracambu" title="Serra do Tiracambu">Serra do Tiracambu</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Serra_das_Alpercatas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Serra das Alpercatas (page does not exist)">Serra das Alpercatas</a>, mark the state's northern margin and the outlines of river valleys.</p>
<p>The climate is hot, and the year is divided into a wet and dry season, extreme humidity being characteristic of the former. The heat, however, is greatly modified on the coast by the south-east trade winds.</p>
<p>The rivers of the state all flow northward to the Atlantic and a majority of them have navigable channels. The <a href="/wiki/Gurupi_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Gurupi River">Gurupi River</a> forms the northwestern boundary of the state, separating Maranhão from neighboring Pará, and the <a href="/wiki/Tocantins_River" title="Tocantins River">Tocantins River</a> forms part the state's southwestern boundary with <a href="/wiki/Tocantins_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Tocantins state">Tocantins state</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Parna%C3%ADba_River" title="Parnaíba River">Parnaíba River</a> forms the eastern boundary of Maranhão, but it has one large tributary, the Balsas, entirely within the state. Other rivers in the state include the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Turiassu_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Turiassu River (page does not exist)">Turiassu</a> (or Turiaçu) which runs just east of the Gurupi, emptying into the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ba%C3%ADa_de_Turiassu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Baía de Turiassu (page does not exist)">Baía de Turiassu</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Mearim_River" title="Mearim River">Mearim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pindar%C3%A9_River" title="Pindaré River">Pindaré</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Graja%C3%BA_River_(Maranh%C3%A3o)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Grajaú River (Maranhão) (page does not exist)">Grajaú</a>, which empty into the <a href="/wiki/Ba%C3%ADa_de_S%C3%A3o_Marcos" title="Baía de São Marcos">Baía de São Marcos</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/Itapecuru_River" title="Itapecuru River">Itapecuru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Munim_River" title="Munim River">Munim</a> which discharge into the <a href="/wiki/Ba%C3%ADa_de_S%C3%A3o_Jos%C3%A9" title="Baía de São José">Baía de São José</a>. Like the Amazon, the Mearim has a <i><a href="/wiki/Pororoca" title="Pororoca">pororoca</a></i> or <a href="/wiki/Tidal_bore" title="Tidal bore">tidal bore</a> in its lower channel, which greatly interferes with navigation.</p>
<p>The western coastline has many small indentations, which are usually masked by islands or shoals. The largest of these are the Baía de Turiassu, facing which is <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Jo%C3%A3o_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="São João Island">São João Island</a>, and the contiguous bays of São Marcos and São José, between which is the large island of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_Island" title="São Luís Island">São Luís</a>. This indented shoreline is home to the <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o_mangroves" title="Maranhão mangroves">Maranhão mangroves</a>, the tallest mangrove forests in the world. The coastline east of Baía de São José is less indented and characterized by sand dunes, including the stark dune fields of the <a href="/wiki/Len%C3%A7%C3%B3is_Maranhenses_National_Park" title="Lençóis Maranhenses National Park">Lençóis Maranhenses National Park</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Restinga" title="Restinga">restinga</a> forests that form on stabilized dunes.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Reviver.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Reviver.JPG/220px-Reviver.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Reviver.JPG/330px-Reviver.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Reviver.JPG/440px-Reviver.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Reviver.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Old <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o" title="São Luís, Maranhão">São Luís</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The first known European to explore Maranhão was the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> explorer <a href="/wiki/Vicente_Y%C3%A1%C3%B1ez_Pinz%C3%B3n" title="Vicente Yáñez Pinzón">Vicente Yáñez Pinzón</a> in 1500<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, but it was granted to <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_de_Barros" title="João de Barros">João de Barros</a> in 1534 as a <a href="/wiki/Captaincies_of_the_Portuguese_Empire" title="Captaincies of the Portuguese Empire">Portuguese hereditary captaincy</a>. The first European settlement, however, was made by a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> trading expedition under <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jacques_Riffault&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jacques Riffault (page does not exist)">Jacques Riffault</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Dieppe,_Seine-Maritime" class="mw-redirect" title="Dieppe, Seine-Maritime">Dieppe</a>, in 1594, who lost two of his three vessels in the vicinity of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_Island" title="São Luís Island">São Luís Island</a>, and left a part of his men on that island when he returned home. Subsequently, Daniel de La Touche, Seigneur de La Rividière was sent to report on the place, and was then commissioned by the French crown to found a colony on the island; this was done in 1612. The French were expelled by the Portuguese in 1615, and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> held the island from 1641 to 1644. In 1621 Ceará, Maranhão and Pará were united and called the "Estado do Maranhao," which was separated from the southern captaincies. Very successful Indian missions were soon begun by the <a href="/wiki/Jesuits" class="mw-redirect" title="Jesuits">Jesuits</a>, who were temporarily expelled as a result of a civil war in 1684 for their opposition to the enslavement of the Indians. Ceará was subsequently detached, but the <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o_(former_state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Maranhão (former state)">State of Maranhão</a> remained separate until 1774, when it again became subject to the colonial administration of Brazil.</p>
<p>In the late 18th century, there was a great influx of enslaved peoples into the region, which corresponded to the increased cultivation of cotton. According to the historian Sven Beckert, the region's cotton exports "doubled between 1770 and 1780, nearly doubled again by 1790, and nearly tripled once more by 1800."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Earl_of_Dundonald_Coat_of_Arms.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Earl_of_Dundonald_Coat_of_Arms.svg/220px-Earl_of_Dundonald_Coat_of_Arms.svg.png" width="220" height="184" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Earl_of_Dundonald_Coat_of_Arms.svg/330px-Earl_of_Dundonald_Coat_of_Arms.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Earl_of_Dundonald_Coat_of_Arms.svg/440px-Earl_of_Dundonald_Coat_of_Arms.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="2485" data-file-height="2080" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Earl_of_Dundonald_Coat_of_Arms.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">Arms</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Dundonald" title="Earl of Dundonald">Marquess of Maranhão (also Earl of Dundonald in Scotland).</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Maranhão did not join in the Brazilian declaration of independence of 1822, but in the following year the Portuguese were driven out by British sailor and liberator Admiral <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Cochrane,_10th_Earl_of_Dundonald" title="Thomas Cochrane, 10th Earl of Dundonald">Lord Cochrane</a> and it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Brazil" title="Empire of Brazil">Empire of Brazil</a>. For this achievement Lord Cochrane became 1st <a href="/wiki/Marques_of_Maranh%C3%A3o" class="mw-redirect" title="Marques of Maranhão">Marques of Maranhão</a> and <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Maranhão Province">Maranhão Province</a>.</p>
<p>São Luís is the Brazilian state capital which most closely resembles a Portuguese city. By the early 20th century São Luís has about 30,000 inhabitants, and contained several convents, charitable institutes, the episcopal palace, a fine Carmelite church, and an ecclesiastical seminary. The historic city center was declared a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> in 1997.São Luís, Maranhão</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Largest_Cities_of_Northeast_Region,_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="Largest Cities of Northeast Region, Brazil">Largest Cities of Northeast Region, Brazil</a></div>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Institute_of_Geography_and_Statistics" title="Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics">IBGE</a> of 2008, there were 6,400,000 people residing in the state. The population density was 18.6 inh./km².</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">Urbanization</a>: 68.1% (2004); <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">Population growth</a>: 1.5% (1991–2000); <a href="/wiki/House" title="House">Houses</a>: 1,442,500 (2005).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The last PNAD (National Research for Sample of Domiciles) census revealed the following numbers: 4,271,000 <a href="/wiki/Brown_people#Pardos_in_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="Brown people">Brown</a> (<a href="/wiki/Multiracial#Brazil" title="Multiracial">Multiracial</a>) people (66.74%), 1,636,000 <a href="/wiki/White_Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="White Brazilian">White</a> people (25.57%), 410,000 <a href="/wiki/Afro-Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="Afro-Brazilian">Black</a> people (6.41%), 43,000 <a href="/wiki/Asian_Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian Brazilian">Asian</a> people (0.67%), 39,000 <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_in_Brazil" title="Indigenous peoples in Brazil">Amerindian</a> people (0.60%).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pal%C3%A1cio_dos_Le%C3%B5es_(noite).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Pal%C3%A1cio_dos_Le%C3%B5es_%28noite%29.jpg/220px-Pal%C3%A1cio_dos_Le%C3%B5es_%28noite%29.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Pal%C3%A1cio_dos_Le%C3%B5es_%28noite%29.jpg/330px-Pal%C3%A1cio_dos_Le%C3%B5es_%28noite%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Pal%C3%A1cio_dos_Le%C3%B5es_%28noite%29.jpg/440px-Pal%C3%A1cio_dos_Le%C3%B5es_%28noite%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2215" data-file-height="1476" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pal%C3%A1cio_dos_Le%C3%B5es_(noite).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Palace" title="Palace">Palace</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyons</a> at night, in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o" title="São Luís, Maranhão">São Luís</a>.</div>

<p>Maranhão is one of the poorest states of Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Service_sector" class="mw-redirect" title="Service sector">service sector</a> is the largest component of <a href="/wiki/GDP" class="mw-redirect" title="GDP">GDP</a> at 54.5%, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_sector" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial sector">industrial sector</a> at 25.4%. <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">Agriculture</a> represents 20.1%, of <a href="/wiki/GDP" class="mw-redirect" title="GDP">GDP</a> (2004). Maranhão exports: <a href="/wiki/Aluminium" title="Aluminium">aluminium</a> 50%, <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a> 23.7%, <a href="/wiki/Soybean" title="Soybean">soybean</a> 13.1% (2002).</p>
<p>Share of the Brazilian economy: 0.9% (2004).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>This State is also known as the land of the palm trees, as the various species of this tree provide its major source of income. The most important of them, from an economic point of view, is the <a href="/wiki/Babassu_oil" title="Babassu oil">babassu</a>. The agribusiness, the aluminium and alumina transformation industries, and the food and timber industries complement the state economy.</p>

But <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> are part of the official <a href="/wiki/High_school" class="mw-redirect" title="High school">high school</a> curriculum.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airports">Airports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Airports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Marechal_Cunha_Machado_International_Airport" title="Marechal Cunha Machado International Airport">Marechal Cunha Machado International Airport</a> is located 13 kilometres (8.1&#160;mi) from the center of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o" title="São Luís, Maranhão">São Luís</a>. It began handling international flights in October 2004. It has a covered area of 8,100 square metres (87,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) and a capacity of one million passengers per year.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Imperatriz_Airport" title="Imperatriz Airport">Renato Moreira Airport</a> is a national airport located in <a href="/wiki/Imperatriz" title="Imperatriz">Imperatriz</a>. <a href="/wiki/Infraero" title="Infraero">Infraero</a> has administered the airport since November 3, 1980, one year before it was officially opened. The passenger terminal was modified and expanded in 1998, giving it new arrival and departure areas, an expanded main concourse, and air conditioning of the entire terminal.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flag">Flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The flag of Maranhão was designed by the poet Joaquim de Souza Andrade, and was adopted by decree nr. 6, of December 21, 1889.</p>
<p>The colored strips symbolize the different ethnic groups which make up the population, and their mixing and living together. The white star in the upper left corner symbolizes Maranhão itself, and is supposed to be <a href="/wiki/Beta_Scorpii" title="Beta Scorpii">Beta Scorpii</a>, as the constellation <a href="/wiki/Scorpius" title="Scorpius">Scorpius</a> is also depicted on the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Brazil" title="Flag of Brazil">national flag of Brazil</a>. The flag has a ratio of 2:3.</p>
