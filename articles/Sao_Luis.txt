<p><b>São Luís</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[sɐ̃w luˈis]</a></span>, <i>Saint Louis</i>) is the capital and largest city of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian state">Brazilian state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o" title="Maranhão">Maranhão</a>. The city is located on <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_island" class="mw-redirect" title="São Luís island">Ilha de São Luís</a> (<i>Saint Louis' Island</i>) in the <a href="/wiki/Ba%C3%ADa_de_S%C3%A3o_Marcos" title="Baía de São Marcos">Baía de São Marcos</a> <i>(Saint Mark's Bay</i>), an extension of the Atlantic Ocean which forms the estuary of <a href="/wiki/Pindar%C3%A9_River" title="Pindaré River">Pindaré</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mearim_River" title="Mearim River">Mearim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Itapecuru_River" title="Itapecuru River">Itapecuru</a> and other rivers. Its coordinates are 2.53° south, 44.30° west. The city proper has a population of some 986,826 people (2008 <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Institute_of_Geography_and_Statistics" title="Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics">IBGE</a> estimate). The metropolitan area totals 1,227,659, ranked as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_cities_in_Brazil" title="List of largest cities in Brazil">16th largest in Brazil</a>.</p>
<p>São Luís, created originally as <i>Saint-Louis</i>, is the only Brazilian state capital founded by France (see <a href="/wiki/France_%C3%89quinoxiale" class="mw-redirect" title="France Équinoxiale">France Équinoxiale</a>) and it is one of the three Brazilian state capitals located on islands (the others are <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria,_Esp%C3%ADrito_Santo" title="Vitória, Espírito Santo">Vitória</a> and <a href="/wiki/Florian%C3%B3polis" title="Florianópolis">Florianópolis</a>).</p>
<p>The city has two major <a href="/wiki/Sea_ports" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea ports">sea ports</a>: <a href="/wiki/Terminal_Mar%C3%ADtimo_de_Ponta_da_Madeira" class="mw-redirect" title="Terminal Marítimo de Ponta da Madeira">Madeira Port</a> and <a href="/wiki/Porto_do_Itaqui" title="Porto do Itaqui">Itaqui Port</a>, through which a substantial part of Brazil's <a href="/wiki/Iron_ore" title="Iron ore">iron ore</a>, originating from the (pre)-<a href="/wiki/Amazon_Basin" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon Basin">Amazon</a> region, is exported. The city's main industries are <a href="/wiki/Metallurgy" title="Metallurgy">metallurgical</a> with <a href="/wiki/Alcoa" title="Alcoa">Alumar</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=VALE&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="VALE (page does not exist)">VALE</a>. São Luís is home of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_University_of_Maranh%C3%A3o" title="Federal University of Maranhão">Federal University of Maranhão</a>.</p>
<p>São Luís was the home town of famous Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Samba" title="Samba">samba</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Alcione_Nazar%C3%A9" class="mw-redirect" title="Alcione Nazaré">Alcione</a>, Brazilian writers <a href="/wiki/Alu%C3%ADsio_Azevedo" title="Aluísio Azevedo">Aluísio Azevedo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferreira_Gullar" title="Ferreira Gullar">Ferreira Gullar</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Josu%C3%A9_Montello&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Josué Montello (page does not exist)">Josué Montello</a>, Belgian-naturalised <a href="/wiki/Soccer" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer">soccer</a> player <a href="/wiki/Luis_Oliveira" class="mw-redirect" title="Luis Oliveira">Luís Oliveira</a>, and the musician João do Vale and <a href="/wiki/Zeca_Baleiro" title="Zeca Baleiro">Zeca Baleiro</a>, a <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BAsica_popular_brasileira" title="Música popular brasileira">Música popular brasileira</a> (MPB) singer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally the town was a large village of the <a href="/wiki/Tupinamb%C3%A1_people" title="Tupinambá people">Tupinambá</a> tribe. The first Europeans to see it were the French, in 1612, who intended to make it the center of a colony named <a href="/wiki/Equinoctial_France" title="Equinoctial France">Equinoctial France</a>. They built a fort named <i>Saint-Louis de Maragnan</i> after <a href="/wiki/Louis_XIII_of_France" title="Louis XIII of France">King Louis XIII</a> and his Saintly ancestor <a href="/wiki/Louis_IX_of_France" title="Louis IX of France">Louis IX</a>. The settlement was conquered for Portugal by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jer%C3%B4nimo_de_Albuquerque&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jerônimo de Albuquerque (page does not exist)">Jerônimo de Albuquerque</a> in 1615, when it was renamed <i>São Luís</i>. There had been little time for the French to build a city, a fact which has led to some controversy as to the actual date of the founding of the city, whether by the French or the Portuguese. In 1641, the city was invaded by the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Brazil" title="Dutch Brazil">Dutch</a>, who left in 1645. In 1677, the city was made the seat of the new <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_do_Maranh%C3%A3o" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of São Luís do Maranhão">Roman Catholic Diocese of São Luís do Maranhão</a>.</p>

<p>Only when those invasions ceased permanently did the colonial government decide to create the state of Grão-Pará e Maranhão, independent from the rest of the country. By that time, the economy was based on agriculture, particularly the exportation of <a href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_bean" title="Cocoa bean">cacao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>. Conflicts amongst the local elites would lead to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Beckman%27s_Revolt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Beckman's Revolt (page does not exist)">Beckman's Revolt</a>. This started due the struggle between jesuits and landowners, the first against the Indian slavery and the others against the unfair treatment given by the Portuguese authorities; the situation lead to an uprising against the Portuguese, led by the brothers Manuel and Tomás Beckman and lasted till the intervention of Portuguese troops under the command of the general Gomes Freire de Andrade. After few skirmishes, the rebels were defeated and the Beckman brothers arrested and, after a brief trial, were <a href="/wiki/Hanged,_drawn_and_quartered" title="Hanged, drawn and quartered">hanged, drawn and quartered</a>. The last words of Manuel Beckman at the gallows were "Pelo Povo do Maranhão morro contente" ("By the Maranhão people I die happy"). The phrase decorates the main hall of State Council Building.</p>
<p>Soon after the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, the region started to provide <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a> to <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">Great Britain</a>. The wealth generated by this activity was used to modernize the city; to bring religious men to come and teach in its schools; and supplement the water supply. The city came to be the third most populous city in the country. By the end of the 19th century, agriculture was in decay and since then the city's population has been searching for other ways to make a living.</p>
<p>Nowadays, São Luís has the largest and best preserved heritage of colonial Portuguese architecture of all Latin America. The island is known as the "Island of Love" and as "the Brazilian Athens", due to its many poets and writers, such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sotero_dos_Reis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sotero dos Reis (page does not exist)">Sotero dos Reis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alu%C3%ADsio_Azevedo" title="Aluísio Azevedo">Aluísio Azevedo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gra%C3%A7a_Aranha" title="Graça Aranha">Graça Aranha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gon%C3%A7alves_Dias" title="Gonçalves Dias">Gonçalves Dias</a> (the most famous), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Josu%C3%A9_Montello&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Josué Montello (page does not exist)">Josué Montello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferreira_Gullar" title="Ferreira Gullar">Ferreira Gullar</a>, among others.</p>
<p>The ancestral composition of São Luís, according to an autosomal DNA study, is 42% European, 39% Native American and 19% African.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>Maranhão's <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> was one of the most prosperous in the country until the mid-nineteenth century. However, after the Civil War the United States of America, when it lost space in the export of cotton, the state collapsed. Only after the end of the 1960s did the state begin to receive incentives and emerge from seclusion, by way of road and rail connections with other regions.</p>
<p>In the late eighteenth century, increased international demand for cotton to meet the English textile industry coupled with reduced production because of the North American War of Independence in the United States provided the perfect setting to stimulate cotton production in Maranhão. Shipping companies and Southampton &amp; Company Maranha Maranha Shipping Company, shipping steam, which performed the transportation of cotton from the states of Georgia and Alabama, began operating in St. Louis shaft - London, leading to production of Caxias and Baixada Maranhão. By the early twentieth century, St. Louis still exporting cotton to England by sea, through the lines and Booth Red Cross Line Line (the extended route to Iquitos) and company-Maranha Liverpool Shipping Company.</p>
<p>During this period, the golden phase of the economy of Maranhão, São Luís has a living cultural effervescence. The city, which related more to the European capitals that the other Brazilian cities, was the first company to receive an Italian opera. Had as few sidewalks and lighting in the country. Received weekly the latest news from French literature. The great fortunes cotton and local business sent their children to study in <a href="/wiki/Recife" title="Recife">Recife</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvador,_Bahia" title="Salvador, Bahia">Salvador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> and, especially, Europe.</p>
<p>The opening of the Port of Itaqui in St. Louis, currently second in depth in the world, second only to the Rotterdam, the Netherlands, and one of the busiest in the country, served to drain the industrial production and iron ore coming from Train Serra dos Carajás, activity operated by Companhia Vale do Rio Doce. The proximity to strategic markets in Europe and North America has led the Port an attractive option to export, but suffers from increased coastal shipping. Ludovicense economy is based on the aluminum processing industry, food, tourism and services. St. Louis has the largest GDP in the state, hosting two public universities (and UFMA UEMA) and various educational institutions and private colleges. According to the latest data from the IBGE survey of the city of St. Louis has a GDP of R $9,340,944,000.00 to 29 and thus the national economy from over 5,560 Brazilian municipalities, and occupying the 14th position among the capitals.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city of São Luís experiences a borderline between <a href="/wiki/Tropical_savanna_climate" title="Tropical savanna climate">tropical savanna climate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tropical_monsoon_climate" title="Tropical monsoon climate">tropical monsoon climate</a>, (<i>Aw</i> and <i>Am</i>) with a short dry season from August to November. During this period when the equatorial rainband associated with the highly seasonalized positioning of the <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a> is not over the city, warm to hot temperatures and abundant equatorial sunshine prevail. Conversely, the humid, rainy wet season dominates the remaining majority of the year, with abundant cloud cover prevalent and heavy rains falling from December to July, with the heaviest rainfall and highest cloud cover from January to May. The cloudiest month of the year is March with a monthly average of 107.2 hours of bright sunshine throughout the month, while the sunniest month of the year is August with a monthly average of 260.3 hours of sunshine throughout the month. The wettest month is April with an average monthly total of 476&#160;mm (18.74&#160;in) of precipitation, while the driest month is October with an average total of average precipitation total of 8&#160;mm (0.31&#160;in). Nearly 70 per cent of average annual precipitation is recorded on average from February to May.</p>
<p>The city is home to the <a href="/wiki/S%C3%ADtio_Rangedor_State_Park" title="Sítio Rangedor State Park">Sítio Rangedor State Park</a>, formerly the fully protected Sítio Rangedor Ecological Station.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The municipality also contains a small part of the <a href="/wiki/Baixada_Maranhense_Environmental_Protection_Area" title="Baixada Maranhense Environmental Protection Area">Baixada Maranhense Environmental Protection Area</a>, a 1,775,035.6 hectares (4,386,208 acres) sustainable use conservation unit created in 1991 that has been a Ramsar Site since 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The 2,634 hectares (6,510 acres) <a href="/wiki/Bacanga_State_Park" title="Bacanga State Park">Bacanga State Park</a> is just south of the city.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>São Luís is known for its tiles which most buildings in the historical centre are covered in. Because of it the city is also known as "The Tiles City".</p>
<p>It also has some cultural peculiarities namely:</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tambor_de_Crioula">Tambor de Crioula</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tambor de Crioula">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Afro-Brazilian dance in which gaily clad women court a bateria of tambors (a row of drums). Whirling and gyrating in time to the music they negotiate for prime position in the centre of the bateria.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tambor_de_Mina">Tambor de Mina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tambor de Mina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Not to be confused with the above, this is the local variant of the Afro-Brazilian religion <a href="/wiki/Candombl%C3%A9" title="Candomblé">Candomblé</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bumba_Meu_Boi">Bumba Meu Boi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Bumba Meu Boi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bumba_Meu_Boi" title="Bumba Meu Boi">Bumba Meu Boi</a> is a popular farce which takes its form as a grand musical pantomime. Practice is a public affair and begins directly after Easter reaching its climax in June when literally hundreds of groups perform on a nightly basis for popular acclaim. Set personalities and characters play out a comedic tragedy with a metaphor for social harmony at its heart. Settlers, the infamous "Coroneis", Indians, spirit workers, African slaves and forest spirits are enacted though costumes, choreography and music - all performed amongst the all-night revelry. The crowd joins in with singing, dancing and dependent on the groups sotaque (or style) the playing of matracas (two wooden blocks, held in each hand and struck together repeatedly). Like the festival of Sao João and its requisite <a href="/wiki/Forr%C3%B3" title="Forró">Forró</a> dance in the North-Eastern states further south Bumba Meu Boi is a harvest festival but with the bull as its centre-piece.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Capoeira">Capoeira</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Capoeira">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The São Luís form of <a href="/wiki/Capoeira" title="Capoeira">capoeira</a> is said to be akin to the kind of capoeira now recognized as 'traditional bahian capoeira' that predated the Bahian Angola/regional polemic which split the capoeira world in the 1950s.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_center">Historical center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Historical center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1997 the city's historical center was made a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>. Starting in 1989 there has been an extensive program to restore and renovate the colonial era buildings of the city's historical center.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:%C3%94nibus_de_S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_2.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/%C3%94nibus_de_S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_2.JPG/200px-%C3%94nibus_de_S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_2.JPG" width="200" height="151" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/%C3%94nibus_de_S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_2.JPG/300px-%C3%94nibus_de_S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_2.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/%C3%94nibus_de_S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_2.JPG/400px-%C3%94nibus_de_S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs_2.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1903" data-file-height="1438" /></a>

<p>The city is served by the <a href="/wiki/Marechal_Cunha_Machado_International_Airport" title="Marechal Cunha Machado International Airport">Marechal Cunha Machado International Airport</a>.</p>
<p>There are 184 bus lines operating in the metropolitan area, served by a fleet of more than 3,000 buses. More than 500,000 people use the bus system every day. The metropolitan area has a network of alternative transportation that works with drivers who usually have their own coach and organize themselves into cooperatives to enable them to provide the public transport service duly legalized in the department of city traffic.</p>
<p>The city has also an efficient radio system with more than 2000 taxi cabs accredited.</p>
<p>Sao Luis is home to a port that receives boats that carry passengers across to the city of Alcantara, which is located in the metropolitan area. It also has another port that receives ferries carrying people and cars that are destined for cities in the region of the state known as the low lands.</p>
<p>Being an island, the city has only one output ground, the bridge of the Estreito dos Mosquitos, which links the island of Sao Luis by road BR-135.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Football is the most popular sport in São Luís, though its clubs aren't much well known in the rest of Brazil. The professional clubs in São Luís are: <a href="/wiki/Sampaio_Corr%C3%AAa_Futebol_Clube" title="Sampaio Corrêa Futebol Clube">Sampaio Corrêa</a> who currently play in Brazil's second division, <a href="/wiki/Moto_Club_de_S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs" title="Moto Club de São Luís">Moto Club</a> who play in the fourth division and <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o_Atl%C3%A9tico_Clube" title="Maranhão Atlético Clube">Maranhão Atlético Clube</a> who take part in the <a href="/wiki/State_football_leagues_in_Brazil" title="State football leagues in Brazil">regional</a> <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Maranhense" title="Campeonato Maranhense">Campeonato Maranhense</a>.</p>
