<p><b>Aisén Province</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Provincia de Aisén</i></span> <small>Pronounced:</small> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/aɪ/ long 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i><b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">EYE</span></span></b>-ssen</i></a></span>) is one of four <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Chile" title="Provinces of Chile">provinces</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Chile" title="Regions of Chile">region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ays%C3%A9n_del_General_Carlos_Ib%C3%A1%C3%B1ez_del_Campo_Region" class="mw-redirect" title="Aysén del General Carlos Ibáñez del Campo Region">Aisen</a> (XI). Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Ais%C3%A9n" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Aisén">Puerto Aisén</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ays%C3%A9n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province had a 2002 population of 23,498 according to the census by the <a href="/wiki/National_Statistics_Institute_(Chile)" title="National Statistics Institute (Chile)">National Statistics Institute</a>. Of these, 16,180 (68.9%) lived in <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban areas</a> and 13,451 (57.2%) in <a href="/wiki/Rural_area" title="Rural area">rural areas</a>. Between the 1992 and 2002 censuses, the population fell by 20.7% ( persons). The census also yielded a surface area of 46,588.8&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (17,988&#160;sq&#160;mi), the largest in the region and fifth largest province in the country, though it is the tenth least populated in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-INE_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-INE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ays%C3%A9n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a province, Palena is a second-level <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Chile" title="Administrative divisions of Chile">administrative division</a>, administered by a presidentially appointed governor. Felisa Ojeda Vargas was appointed by <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> <a href="/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A1n_Pi%C3%B1era" title="Sebastián Piñera">Sebastián Piñera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Munitel_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Munitel-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Communes">Communes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ays%C3%A9n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Communes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The province comprises three <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">communes</a>, each governed by a municipality, headed by an <a href="/wiki/Alcalde" title="Alcalde">alcalde</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ais%C3%A9n,_Chile" class="mw-redirect" title="Aisén, Chile">Aisén</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cisnes" title="Cisnes">Cisnes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Guaitecas" title="Guaitecas">Guaitecas</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_name">Origin of name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ays%C3%A9n_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Origin of name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The name <i>Aisén</i> is from the English sailor expression for the icy "end of the world". Many of the region's people are of <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_German" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic German">German</a> descent, although the majority of inhabitants are Chileans of <a href="/wiki/Mestizo" title="Mestizo">mestizo</a> <a href="/wiki/Spanish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> origins. The province was recently developed in the early 20th century by Chilean government officials to place thousands of transplanted settlers from the <a href="/wiki/Central_Valley_of_Chile" class="mw-redirect" title="Central Valley of Chile">Central Valley</a>.</p>
